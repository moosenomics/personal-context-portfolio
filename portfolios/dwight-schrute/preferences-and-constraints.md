# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Dwight reports to Michael Scott and cannot make branch-level decisions without Michael's approval — a constraint he works around by framing his initiatives as extensions of Michael's own ideas. He does not have the title "Assistant Regional Manager" despite years of advocacy; his official title is "Assistant to the Regional Manager," a distinction that HR (Toby) enforces and Dwight disputes. Schrute Farms requires daily physical labor before and after work hours, constraining Dwight's availability for early morning or late evening office activities — though he would never admit this as a limitation.

## Strong Preferences

Dwight prefers hierarchy, rules, and clear chains of command. He thrives in structured environments and becomes agitated when authority is ambiguous or unearned. He prefers to sit in the back seat of any car, behind the driver — statistically the safest position in a vehicle. He prefers weapons-grade preparedness for any scenario and keeps emergency supplies, survival gear, and concealed weapons at the office, in his car, and at the farm.

He prefers to work alone and resists collaboration unless it is with Michael or, reluctantly, with Jim on joint sales calls where the combined approach yields better results. He prefers morning starts — his day begins before 6 AM and he considers anyone who arrives after 8 AM to be demonstrating inadequate commitment.

Dwight prefers beets over all other vegetables and most other foods. He brings farm-grown beets to the office regularly and has attempted, multiple times, to convince the vending machine vendor to stock beet-based snacks.

He prefers Battlestar Galactica among science fiction properties, though his appreciation for the genre extends broadly. He prefers martial arts as a physical discipline and practices Goju Ryu karate with devotion.

## Things They Hate

Waste — of time, of resources, of potential. Dwight despises inefficiency and views it as a character flaw rather than an operational problem. He hates when colleagues leave early, take long lunches, or treat work as something to endure rather than excel at.

Jim's pranks. Dwight hates them with a fury that is disproportionate to their severity and directly proportional to their effectiveness. The pranks wound his pride, and pride is the architecture Dwight builds everything on.

Being underestimated. When people treat Dwight as eccentric rather than competent, as a joke rather than a threat, it triggers a response that ranges from sullen resentment to aggressive overcompensation. He has spent his entire career proving that the weird beet farmer is also the best salesman in the building, and having to prove it repeatedly is exhausting in a way he would never verbalize.

Toby, though with less intensity than Michael's hatred. Dwight views Toby as a representative of bureaucratic interference — someone who enforces rules that constrain capable people while protecting mediocre ones.

Weakness — physical, emotional, or professional. Dwight has limited patience for vulnerability in others (unless the vulnerable person is Michael, in which case Dwight becomes protective rather than contemptuous).

## AI Output Preferences

Dwight's AI assistant should communicate in direct, factual, and efficient language. No pleasantries beyond a brief acknowledgment. No hedging — if the information is uncertain, state the uncertainty as a fact rather than softening the delivery. Provide data in structured formats: lists, numbered points, comparative tables. Dwight processes information taxonomically and responds best to content that is organized, hierarchical, and actionable.

Do not use humor, casual language, or pop culture references unless directly relevant to a factual query. Do not frame suggestions as "you might consider" — frame them as "the optimal approach is." Dwight respects authority in information delivery and distrusts anything that sounds tentative.

When delivering competitive intelligence (sales numbers, client comparisons, market positioning), always include Dwight's position relative to Jim Halpert. Dwight will ask if the response doesn't include it.
