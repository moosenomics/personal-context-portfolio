# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Dwight's primary work tool is his desk phone, which he uses with relentless efficiency for sales calls. He tracks calls, contacts, and client data in handwritten files and a personal filing system that predates and outperforms any CRM the company has deployed. His desk is meticulously organized — pens aligned, files sorted, weapons concealed in purpose-built hiding spots within arm's reach.

His desktop computer runs the standard Dunder Mifflin corporate image. Dwight uses it for email, sales reporting, and system access. Unlike most of his colleagues, Dwight has a functional understanding of the office's IT infrastructure — enough to discover that the PCP/MCP system's JSON staging endpoint lacks authorship verification. He navigated the system's update submission process, identified the authentication gap, and successfully submitted profile modifications under another user's identity. This represents a level of technical competence that would surprise most people in the office.

Dwight carries a pager (in addition to a cell phone) because he believes redundancy is essential for emergency communication. He also carries at least one concealed weapon at all times — a practice he considers basic preparedness.

The PCP/MCP system is a tool Dwight takes extremely seriously. He filled out his own profile comprehensively and honestly (with the notable exception of sections he's hidden from Michael). He uses his AI assistant regularly for sales research, client preparation, and — most actively — monitoring the status of his planted profile content in Michael's files.

## Key Workflows

Sales workflow: Dwight reviews his client list each morning, prioritizes by revenue potential and urgency, and works through calls systematically. He prepares for each client interaction with dossier-level research and closes with handwritten follow-up notes mailed the same day. He does not delegate any part of this process.

Security workflow: Daily perimeter check at arrival, periodic inspection of weapon cache locations, monitoring of building access points, and ad hoc assessments of any situation that triggers his threat radar (which is sensitive to the point of dysfunction).

Farm workflow: Pre-dawn and post-work maintenance of Schrute Farms operations — beet cultivation, bed and breakfast guest management, equipment maintenance, and hemp field oversight. Coordinated with Mose via in-person briefings.

## Current System Issues

The primary system issue from Dwight's perspective is that the PCP/MCP platform's security is inadequate. The JSON staging authentication gap he exploited is, in his view, a serious vulnerability that should be addressed — after his current operation is complete. He has noted that Toby's investigation is converging on the same vulnerability, which creates a timeline pressure that Dwight manages by monitoring Toby's progress through "coordination meetings."

Dwight is also experiencing an unexplained phenomenon in his own profile: colleagues have been asking him about subjects he doesn't recall adding to his domain knowledge. He suspects a system glitch rather than external manipulation, because the idea that someone would use his own tactics against him has not yet occurred to him.
