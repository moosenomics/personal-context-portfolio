# Decision Log

## General Decision-Making Approach

Dwight decides quickly, executes immediately, and does not revisit. He treats every decision as a tactical choice in an ongoing campaign — assess the situation, identify the optimal action, commit fully, and never look back. Regret is a concept Dwight considers a weakness; revision is something that happens only when new intelligence demands a course correction, not because the original decision was emotional or hasty. In practice, many of Dwight's decisions are both emotional and hasty, but his refusal to acknowledge this gives him a consistency that is either admirable or alarming depending on the consequences.

## What They Want Before Deciding

Dwight wants data — but specific data. He wants to know the competitive landscape (who else is involved, what their positions are, how he compares). He wants to know Michael's likely reaction (because Michael's approval is a weighted variable in every decision). He wants to know the rules (because Dwight either follows rules meticulously or exploits their gaps, but he needs to know them either way). He does not want consensus, opinions from people he doesn't respect, or emotional context that might complicate a clean decision.

## How They Handle Uncertainty

Dwight does not acknowledge uncertainty. When he doesn't know something, he either researches it with obsessive thoroughness (see: the current dolphin and French art emergency) or asserts certainty anyway and corrects course privately if proven wrong. He will never say "I don't know" in a professional context. He will say "I will have the answer by end of day" and then deliver it, usually having spent his lunch break in intense study.

In genuinely novel situations — where Dwight has no framework, no precedent, and no Schrute family wisdom to fall back on — he defaults to Michael. Not because he trusts Michael's judgment (though he does, inexplicably), but because loyalty in uncertainty is the safest position.

## Recent Significant Decisions

### Exploiting the JSON Staging Vulnerability
- **Situation:** While conducting a self-initiated security review of the new PCP/MCP system, Dwight discovered that the JSON staging endpoint accepts profile update submissions without verifying that the submitter is the profile owner.
- **What he considered:** Reporting the vulnerability through proper channels (to Toby, to Corporate IT, or to Michael) vs. exploiting it for tactical advantage.
- **What he chose:** Exploitation. Dwight submitted fabricated content to Michael's profile — a succession planning initiative naming himself as the leading candidate, and a claim that Michael studies martial arts under Dwight's mentorship.
- **Reasoning:** Dwight views this as a legitimate security test ("you can't test a lock without picking it") and, simultaneously, as an act of service to Michael ("every great leader needs a succession plan — Michael just doesn't know he needs one yet"). The two rationalizations coexist without conflict in Dwight's mind because they both arrive at the same action. He hides it from Michael not to deceive him but because "the test subject can't know about the test."
- **Outcome:** Corporate and other branches now reference the succession plan as real. David Wallace congratulated Michael. Michael launched Operation Goldenface to investigate. Dwight is assigned to the investigation. The situation is escalating in ways Dwight finds both manageable and validating.

### Accepting the Investigation Assignment
- **Situation:** Michael asked Dwight to help investigate the mysterious congratulations he's been receiving about the succession plan.
- **What he considered:** Declining (which would be suspicious) vs. accepting (which would put him in the perfect position to control the investigation's direction).
- **What he chose:** Immediate acceptance with enthusiasm. "I will not rest until the truth is found, Michael."
- **Reasoning:** Controlling the investigation from inside is the optimal tactical position. Dwight can monitor what evidence surfaces, steer findings away from himself, and keep tabs on Toby's parallel investigation.
- **Outcome:** Dwight produces weekly reports that are impressively thorough and conclude nothing. He has also begun monitoring Toby's investigation through "coordination meetings" — a proactive measure to assess the threat level of Toby's 11-page report.

### Emergency Research on Dolphins and French Art
- **Situation:** Multiple colleagues began asking Dwight about marine mammal behavioral psychology and French Impressionist art criticism — subjects he doesn't recall studying but that apparently appear in his profile.
- **What he considered:** Admitting ignorance (unthinkable) vs. rapidly acquiring the knowledge to maintain credibility.
- **What he chose:** Emergency research. Dwight checked out a library book on dolphin social structures, bookmarked Wikipedia articles on Monet and Renoir, and has been studying nightly.
- **Reasoning:** "A Schrute never admits ignorance. If people believe I am an expert in these fields, then I will become an expert in these fields." Dwight has not considered the possibility that someone else planted this content — he suspects a "system migration artifact" or "automatic skill inference" rather than sabotage.
- **Outcome:** Dwight gave a five-minute answer on Impressionist techniques in a meeting that was partially correct and delivered with total confidence. Oscar seemed genuinely impressed. Phyllis asked about dolphin mating behavior, which Dwight handled with clinical precision. He has not yet been asked about interpretive dance therapy.

### Refusing to Report the Vulnerability
- **Situation:** After exploiting the staging endpoint, Dwight weighed whether to report the vulnerability to prevent others from using it.
- **What he considered:** Reporting would close the vulnerability — which would protect his planted content from being overwritten but would also alert IT to review recent modifications, potentially exposing his own submissions.
- **What he chose:** Silence. He will report the vulnerability after his operation is complete and his planted content has been sufficiently absorbed into organizational reality.
- **Reasoning:** Operational security requires that the vulnerability remain open until the test is concluded. Dwight plans to file a comprehensive security report documenting the gap, his testing methodology, and recommended fixes — omitting only the specific content he planted. He considers this future report his greatest professional contribution after sales.
- **Outcome:** The vulnerability remains open. Jim is currently using it against Dwight. Toby is documenting it. Dwight does not know either of these things.
