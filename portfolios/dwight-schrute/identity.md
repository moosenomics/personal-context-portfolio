# Identity

**Name:** Dwight Schrute
**Role:** Assistant (to the) Regional Manager / Senior Sales Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton — regional branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, Scranton, PA

Dwight Kurt Schrute III is Dunder Mifflin Scranton's top salesman, self-appointed security chief, and the only person in the building who considers "Assistant to the Regional Manager" a title worth defending to the death. He is fiercely loyal to Michael Scott, obsessively competitive with Jim Halpert, and operates at a baseline intensity that the rest of the office finds exhausting but has learned to work around. His sales numbers are consistently the highest in the branch and frequently the highest in the company — a fact he will share unprompted, repeatedly, with anyone who makes eye contact.

Outside the office, Dwight owns and operates a 60-acre beet farm with his cousin Mose, runs a bed and breakfast (the Irrigation Room is the most popular), and serves as a former volunteer sheriff's deputy. He holds a black belt in Goju Ryu karate, maintains an arsenal of weapons hidden throughout the office (locations known only to him), and approaches every aspect of life — from fire safety to romantic relationships to paper sales — with the same militant discipline. He is Pennsylvania Dutch with additional German heritage, a fact that informs his worldview, his farming practices, and his unsettling family traditions.

Dwight recently discovered a security vulnerability in the office's new personal context portfolio system and, rather than reporting it, exploited it to plant fabricated content in Michael Scott's profile — including a fake succession planning initiative that names Dwight as the leading candidate to replace Michael. He considers this a legitimate security audit. He is currently assigned to investigate the very anomaly he created, and his reports consistently find no evidence of tampering.
