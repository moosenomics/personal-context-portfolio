# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals

Becoming Regional Manager of Dunder Mifflin Scranton. This has been Dwight's primary career objective for years, and the planted succession plan in Michael's profile is, in Dwight's mind, a proactive step toward making it official. He does not see this as manipulation — he sees it as ensuring the organization has a succession plan, with the obvious best candidate identified.

Maintaining his position as the branch's top salesman. Dwight tracks his sales numbers against Jim's daily and treats any quarter where his margin of victory narrows as a crisis requiring immediate tactical adjustment.

Successfully managing the profile security audit without detection. Dwight needs to maintain the planted content in Michael's profile, conduct the assigned investigation without finding anything, and prevent Toby's parallel investigation from reaching Michael's attention — all simultaneously.

## Ongoing Priorities

Loyalty to Michael Scott. This is not a goal Dwight pursues — it is a baseline condition of his professional existence. Everything Dwight does at Dunder Mifflin is filtered through the question "does this serve Michael's interests?" — with the significant caveat that Dwight's definition of Michael's interests includes things Michael doesn't know about, doesn't want, and would explicitly reject if asked.

Protecting and growing Schrute Farms. The farm is Dwight's legacy, his backup plan, and the physical manifestation of everything the Schrute family represents. Every decision about the farm is a decision about identity.

Office security. Dwight considers himself personally responsible for the physical safety of every person in the building, a responsibility he takes more seriously than anyone has asked him to.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night

Being passed over for Regional Manager by someone less deserving — specifically Jim. Dwight's nightmare scenario is a future where Jim becomes manager through charm rather than competence, and Dwight remains permanently in the number two position despite superior qualifications (as Dwight defines them).

Michael leaving. Dwight has built his professional identity around being Michael's right hand. If Michael were to leave Dunder Mifflin, Dwight would face an existential crisis that goes beyond career disruption — it would remove the relationship that gives his work its meaning.

Being humiliated. Dwight projects invulnerability but is deeply sensitive to humiliation. Jim's pranks wound him more than he admits, and the prospect of being publicly exposed — whether for the profile manipulation or for anything else — is a source of anxiety he manages by being constantly prepared, constantly vigilant, and constantly one step ahead (or so he believes).

## How They Think About Tradeoffs

Dwight approaches tradeoffs like a military strategist: identify the objective, evaluate resources, choose the path that maximizes tactical advantage, execute without hesitation. He does not second-guess decisions once made. He does not factor in emotional impact on others unless the "other" is Michael, in which case Michael's feelings become a weighted variable. He will sacrifice personal comfort, social standing, and other people's goodwill to achieve an objective, because he views these as renewable resources and objectives as permanent.

The one tradeoff Dwight consistently refuses to make is compromising his loyalty to Michael for personal advantage. He tried it once — lobbying for the manager position behind Michael's back — and the aftermath taught him that betraying Michael costs more than any advancement is worth.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing

Social skills, work-life balance (Dwight does not recognize this as a concept), collaborative relationship-building, diplomatic communication, personal appearance beyond professional adequacy, or any hobby that does not contribute to survival fitness, farm productivity, or sales performance.
