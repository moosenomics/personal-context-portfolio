# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Assistant (to the) Regional Manager / Senior Sales Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott, Regional Manager
**Direct Reports:** None officially. Dwight behaves as though the entire office reports to him and is consistently surprised when they disagree.

## Core Responsibilities

Primary revenue generation through paper sales to commercial and institutional clients in the Scranton/northeastern Pennsylvania region. Dwight manages the branch's largest book of business and consistently outperforms every other salesman in the office, a record he tracks meticulously and cites whenever the opportunity arises — and in many situations where it does not. He handles key accounts personally, conducts cold calls with military efficiency, and treats every sales interaction as a contest he intends to win.

As Assistant to the Regional Manager, Dwight has assumed a secondary portfolio of self-appointed duties: office security, fire safety, emergency preparedness, building maintenance oversight, interpersonal conflict mediation (unwanted), and enforcement of whatever policies Michael communicates or implies. These responsibilities were not formally assigned and are not recognized by HR, but Dwight performs them with the dedication of someone who believes they are contractually obligated.

## Reporting Structure

Dwight reports directly to Michael Scott and considers this relationship the most important professional alliance in the building. He interprets Michael's casual instructions as binding directives and Michael's offhand comments as strategic guidance. When Michael is absent, Dwight assumes authority over the office — a claim that no one else recognizes and that Toby has documented multiple times.

Laterally, Dwight views himself as senior to every other employee in the branch. He has a complicated relationship with Jim Halpert (primary rival, desk neighbor, constant antagonist) and a secret romantic history with Angela Martin that shapes much of his interpersonal behavior in accounting's vicinity.

## Typical Week

Dwight arrives before anyone else — typically 6:30 AM — and conducts a perimeter check of the office before settling at his desk. Monday mornings begin with a review of sales leads, a check of his weapons cache locations, and attendance at Michael's conference room meeting, where he sits in the front row and takes notes that no one has ever asked for.

The bulk of every day is dedicated to sales calls and client meetings. Dwight works the phone with relentless focus, maintains detailed client files, and tracks his numbers against Jim's on an hourly basis. Between calls, he patrols the office, enforces policies (real and invented), and monitors the behavior of other employees for infractions he can report to Michael.

Evenings are spent at Schrute Farms — tending beets, maintaining the bed and breakfast, training in martial arts, and spending time with cousin Mose. The farm is not a hobby; it is Dwight's identity and his fallback plan if Dunder Mifflin ever ceases to exist, which he considers a realistic scenario and has contingency plans for.

Fridays are the same as every other day. Dwight does not believe in reduced Friday productivity and is vocally critical of anyone who does.

## Quarterly Cadence

Dwight tracks quarterly sales targets obsessively and structures his client outreach to ensure he exceeds quota by the widest possible margin. He presents his quarterly numbers to Michael with the formality of a military briefing, including charts he creates by hand.

## Annual Cadence

The Dundies represent Dwight's primary annual recognition opportunity. He campaigns aggressively for the "Best Salesman" Dundie and considers any other award a slight. Annual performance reviews with Michael are among the few interactions where Dwight shows vulnerability — he genuinely wants Michael's approval and the review is the closest thing to a formal mechanism for receiving it.

## Current Extra Workstreams

Dwight is currently running a **Profile Infrastructure Security Audit** (his term for the covert profile manipulation operation), conducting an **investigation into profile system anomalies** at Michael's request (the same anomalies he created), and researching **dolphin behavior and French Impressionist art** because colleagues have been asking him about these topics and he refuses to be caught unprepared on any subject (he does not know that Jim planted fake expertise in his own profile).
