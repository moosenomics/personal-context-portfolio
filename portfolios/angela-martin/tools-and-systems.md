# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Angela's primary work tool is the accounting software and spreadsheet suite on her desktop computer. She maintains the branch's financial records with a precision that borders on devotional — every transaction categorized, every reconciliation documented, every discrepancy traced to its source (usually Kevin). Her filing system is both digital and physical, with paper backups stored in labeled folders organized by quarter, category, and importance.

She uses a desk calculator for quick verifications, even when the same calculation could be done in the spreadsheet, because she trusts her own arithmetic and considers it a professional discipline to check work by hand. Her desk is immaculate — no clutter, no personal items beyond a small framed photo of her cats and a poster of babies posing as adults that has been the subject of multiple HR complaints from Oscar.

Angela monitors her cats remotely via a home camera system accessible from her phone. She checks the feed during breaks and occasionally during meetings, though she would deny the latter if asked.

The PCP/MCP system is a tool Angela has adopted with characteristic thoroughness. She filled out her profile completely and implemented the most complex redaction scheme in the office — multiple include/exclude blocks at varying access levels. She uses her AI assistant primarily for PPC planning (vendor research, event logistics) and accounting reference (policy lookups, tax code clarifications). She has filed a formal feature request for per-paragraph access controls, which she considers essential for proper information classification.

## Key Workflows

Accounting workflow: Daily transaction review, weekly reconciliation, monthly close, quarterly reporting. Angela processes the branch's financial data in a rigid sequence that has not changed since she established it. Oscar participates in the workflow; Kevin generates items that enter the workflow as errors to be corrected.

PPC workflow: Event planning follows Angela's proprietary process — theme selection (Angela), vendor engagement (Angela), decoration specification (Angela, with Phyllis executing), food ordering (Angela, vegetarian options mandatory), post-event evaluation (Angela, written and filed). The binder containing PPC documentation is the most comprehensive non-financial record in the branch.

## Current System Issues

The PPC/MCP system lacks the granularity Angela requires. She wants per-paragraph redaction controls, not section-level blocks. Her current workaround is to structure her content so that each paragraph that requires different access is in its own section with its own markers — a functional but inelegant solution that she has documented in her formal complaint to HR.

Angela has also noted that Kevin, despite being in the accounting department, should not have access to certain financial details that the `accounting` tag currently makes visible to him. She considers Kevin's tag membership an access control error and has requested that it be reviewed — a request that is structurally impossible under the current system's tag model, which does not support excluding individual members from their own role group (hence the nested exclude blocks Angela has implemented as a workaround).
