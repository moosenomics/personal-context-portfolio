# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise

**Branch-level accounting and financial management.** Angela's knowledge of accounting practices, tax requirements, expense categorization, and financial reporting is comprehensive and current. She understands Dunder Mifflin's chart of accounts intimately, can identify categorization errors by inspection, and maintains the branch's books at a standard that has never drawn a corporate correction. Her expertise extends beyond bookkeeping to financial analysis — she can read a P&L, identify trending variances, and contextualize branch performance within the company's broader financial picture.

**Event planning and logistics.** Angela has planned and executed dozens of office events — birthdays, holidays, corporate celebrations, and the Dundies. Her knowledge covers vendor management, budget optimization, theme development, catering logistics, and the politics of office celebrations (who gets acknowledged, how, and at what cost). She runs events with the precision of a project manager and the aesthetic sensibility of someone who has strong opinions about streamer colors and will not compromise on them.

**Access control and information classification.** Angela's implementation of the PCP redaction system is the most sophisticated in the office. She understands the marker syntax, nesting rules, and tag model well enough to construct multi-tiered access hierarchies that separate public content from committee-level, department-level, and personal content. Her formal complaint about the system's granularity limitations demonstrates that she has thought about information classification more deeply than anyone else in the building, including the people who built the system.

**Cat care and feline behavior.** Angela's knowledge of cat breeds, nutrition, medical care, behavioral patterns, and socialization is extensive and continuously updated. She can discuss feline health with the authority of a veterinary technician and the devotion of someone for whom cats are not pets but family members of higher standing than most of her coworkers.

**Conservative Christian theology and ethics (selectively applied).** Angela has a firm grounding in the religious framework that organizes her worldview — moral absolutes, traditional values, personal accountability, and the conviction that most people are falling short. The selectivity of application is not something Angela acknowledges.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner

**Emotional self-awareness.** Angela does not examine her own motivations, contradictions, or emotional needs with any regularity. She operates on a set of stated principles and does not conduct audits of whether her behavior aligns with them — an irony, given her professional commitment to reconciliation and accuracy.

**Collaborative leadership.** Angela leads through directive authority, not through consensus, motivation, or empowerment. She does not solicit input, does not delegate decision-making, and does not invest in developing the skills of the people who report to her (Kevin might improve with different management; Angela has never tested this hypothesis). Her leadership style works for accounting because the work is structured and measurable, but it would fail in any context requiring buy-in or voluntary effort.

**Technology beyond end-user tools.** Angela uses accounting software and the PCP system competently but has no understanding of the underlying infrastructure. She could not diagnose a technical issue, configure a system setting, or evaluate the security architecture she has built her classification scheme on top of.

## Frameworks / Mental Models

Angela's primary mental model is **classification.** Everything belongs in a category — expenses, people, behaviors, colors, moral positions. Once classified, things are handled according to the rules that apply to their category. This model produces excellent accounting and rigid interpersonal behavior in equal measure.

Her secondary model is **hierarchy.** Every relationship has a rank order — she is senior to Kevin, peer to Oscar (reluctantly), subordinate to Michael (procedurally), and authority over the PPC (absolutely). She navigates the office by understanding where she stands relative to everyone else and behaving accordingly. Challenges to her hierarchical position are treated as system errors to be corrected.

Her tertiary model is **propriety.** There is a right way to behave, and Angela knows what it is. This model generates most of her judgments and all of her hypocrisy — because the model does not include a self-audit function, Angela can simultaneously condemn office romances and conduct a secret affair without experiencing cognitive dissonance. The contradiction exists; the awareness of it does not.
