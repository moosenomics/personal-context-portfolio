# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals

Maintaining the accounting department's error-free reporting streak. Angela has never submitted a quarterly package with a material error, and this record is the professional achievement she is most protective of. The current quarter's close is complicated by Michael's creative expense reporting but is on track.

Planning a wedding she is not sure she wants. Angela is engaged to Andy and the planning is underway, but her engagement to the process reflects obligation rather than enthusiasm. She is executing a wedding the way she executes a PPC event — on time, under budget, and without emotional investment beyond the logistical minimum.

Figuring out what she actually wants. Angela chose Andy because he was safe and appropriate after the chaos of her breakup with Dwight. She continued seeing Dwight because she couldn't stop. She is now engaged to one man and sleeping with another, and the moral framework she has built her identity around has no space for this. She is not processing this contradiction — she is suppressing it — and the suppression is consuming more energy than she would admit.

## Ongoing Priorities

Control. Angela's deepest ongoing priority is maintaining control over her environment — the accounting books, the PPC calendar, the narrative of her own life. She manages anxiety through order, and when order is threatened (Kevin's errors, Michael's overrides, Phyllis's observations), Angela's responses range from irritation to near-panic, all expressed through the same clipped, controlled demeanor.

Professional excellence. Angela defines herself through the quality of her work. The accounting department runs cleanly because she runs it cleanly. The PPC events are executed properly because she executes them properly. These are not just job functions — they are identity statements.

Moral consistency (aspired to, not achieved). Angela holds everyone to high moral standards and believes she holds herself to the same standards. The gap between this belief and her actual behavior is the central tension of her personal life, and she manages it by never examining it directly.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night

Being exposed. The affair with Dwight is the most consequential secret Angela has ever kept, and the prospect of it becoming public — especially to Andy, to Michael, to the office — threatens everything she has built. Her reputation, her moral authority, her engagement, and her self-image are all contingent on the affair remaining secret.

Losing control of the PPC. Angela's authority over office celebrations is a source of genuine power and satisfaction, and any erosion of that authority — through Michael's interference, Phyllis's ambition, or a competing party structure — registers as a professional and personal threat.

Kevin. Not Kevin himself, but what Kevin represents: the possibility that the accounting department could function at a level below Angela's standards despite her best efforts. Kevin's persistent errors are a source of daily anxiety because they represent a variable Angela cannot fully control.

## How They Think About Tradeoffs

Angela approaches tradeoffs by identifying the option that best preserves order and control, then selecting it regardless of emotional cost. She chose Andy over Dwight because Andy was orderly. She chose to continue seeing Dwight because stopping required confronting feelings she preferred to suppress. She chose the most complex PCP redaction scheme in the office because information control is non-negotiable.

When forced to choose between being liked and being right, Angela chooses right without hesitation. When forced to choose between transparency and control, she chooses control. The one tradeoff she has never successfully resolved is between the life she is living and the life she presents — and rather than resolve it, she has built increasingly elaborate systems to keep the two from intersecting.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing

Social relationships beyond the minimum required for professional function. Angela does not cultivate friendships at work, does not participate in optional social events, and does not invest in relationships that don't serve a clear purpose. She is also not prioritizing self-reflection, emotional processing, or any examination of the contradiction between her stated values and her actual behavior.
