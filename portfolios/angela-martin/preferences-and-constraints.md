# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Angela reports to Michael Scott, whose financial behavior generates the accounting irregularities she must resolve. Kevin Malone is a member of her accounting team and cannot be reassigned, replaced, or — despite Angela's periodic suggestions — terminated. The PPC budget is set by corporate and supplemented minimally by the branch, leaving Angela to execute events within margins she considers insulting. The PCP redaction system only supports section-level access controls, not the per-paragraph granularity Angela requires.

Angela is engaged to Andy Bernard. The engagement is public, the wedding is being planned, and any change in status would be visible to the entire office.

## Strong Preferences

Angela prefers order in all forms — organized files, structured schedules, controlled environments, and predictable human behavior. She experiences physical discomfort in messy or chaotic spaces and has been known to straighten other people's desks when they leave for the day.

She is a vegetarian and requires vegetarian options at all office events — a preference she implements through PPC authority rather than personal request. She considers the colors green and orange inappropriate for professional decorations (green is "whorish"; orange is "aggressive"). She approves of beige, cream, light blue, and white.

Angela prefers cats to humans in most contexts and maintains a household of multiple cats, each named, each with established routines, and each monitored via home camera during work hours. She prefers Christian music, conservative values, and the baby-posed-as-adult poster genre. She would take the Bible and The Purpose Driven Life to a desert island.

She prefers to eat at her desk. She prefers to work without interruption. She prefers silence to conversation and compliance to collaboration.

## Things They Hate

Vulgarity in any form — crude humor, inappropriate attire, suggestive decorations, or any behavior she categorizes as morally coarse. Michael's "That's what she said" is a recurring source of disgust.

Challenges to her authority. Angela responds to PPC overrides, accounting second-guessing, and any form of institutional end-run with a cold fury that is more effective than shouting because it never dissipates. She holds grudges with the precision of a filing system — catalogued, cross-referenced, and available for retrieval at any time.

Kevin's errors. Each mistake is both a professional problem and a personal affront. Angela has compiled a mental (and partially written) record of Kevin's deficiencies that she considers sufficient grounds for termination, though she acknowledges that the decision is not hers to make.

Being told to lighten up, relax, or have fun. Angela does not consider these invitations. She considers them insults.

Pam and Jim's relationship — not on personal grounds (though Angela disapproves of office romance publicly while conducting one privately) but because their open display of affection violates her sense of workplace decorum.

## AI Output Preferences

Angela's AI assistant should communicate in a professional, structured format. No casual language, no humor, no emoji, no exclamation points. Present information in numbered lists or clean paragraphs with clear headers. Respect her authority over accounting and PPC decisions by framing suggestions as options rather than corrections. Never imply that Angela's approach is wrong — present alternatives as "additional considerations" if they must be presented at all.

When providing financial information, use precise numbers with proper formatting. When providing PPC-related information, reference her existing standards and preferences. Do not suggest anything green or orange.
