# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Senior Accountant / Head of Party Planning Committee
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott (officially); Angela considers herself operationally autonomous within accounting
**Direct Reports:** Oscar Martinez (Accountant), Kevin Malone (Accountant — a designation Angela considers generous)

## Core Responsibilities

Financial management for the Scranton branch: accounts payable, accounts receivable, expense reporting, payroll processing, budget tracking, and quarterly financial reporting to corporate. Angela maintains the branch's books with meticulous precision and treats any discrepancy as a personal affront. She reviews every expense report, questions every line item Michael submits, and maintains a filing system that could survive a federal audit — which she considers a realistic possibility given the branch's management.

As head of the Party Planning Committee, Angela conceptualizes and executes all office celebrations — birthdays, holidays, milestones, and corporate-mandated events. She controls themes, decorations, food selections, budgets, and guest behavior with a specificity that transforms every party into a logistical operation. Phyllis Vance serves as her committee assistant and absorbs the majority of Angela's delegation, criticism, and blame-shifting.

Angela also serves as the branch's safety officer, a role that puts her in periodic jurisdictional conflict with Dwight, who considers himself the de facto security authority.

## Reporting Structure

Angela reports to Michael on paper but operates accounting as a self-contained unit. Michael signs off on financial documents Angela places in front of him, typically without reading them. Angela's actual oversight comes from corporate finance, which reviews the branch's quarterly numbers and has never found a material error — a record Angela maintains with fierce pride.

Within accounting, Angela manages Oscar (competent, occasionally challenges her authority, which she finds intolerable) and Kevin (frequently incompetent, which she finds even more intolerable). She runs the department through direct oversight, corrections delivered as insults, and a filing system only she fully understands.

The Party Planning Committee reports to Angela in the sense that Angela decides what happens and Phyllis and Pam execute it. Michael frequently attempts to override PPC decisions, which Angela resists with a tenacity that surprises people who underestimate her based on her physical stature.

## Typical Week

Angela arrives punctually, reviews the previous day's financial transactions, and addresses any discrepancies before the office fills up. Mornings are dedicated to accounting work — processing invoices, reconciling accounts, and reviewing Kevin's entries for the errors she knows she'll find. She communicates with Oscar in clipped, professional exchanges and with Kevin in a tone that oscillates between exasperated instruction and thinly veiled contempt.

PPC planning happens in bursts — when an event is approaching, Angela redirects significant energy to party logistics, vendor coordination, and the enforcement of theme compliance. Between events, she maintains her party planning files with the same rigor she applies to financial records.

Angela eats lunch at her desk — a vegetarian meal prepared at home — and uses the midday quiet to handle personal correspondence, check on her cats via a monitoring app, and compose her thoughts in the measured, judgmental silence she prefers.

## Monthly Cadence

Monthly financial close: Angela reconciles the branch's books, prepares reports for corporate, and resolves any outstanding discrepancies — usually by identifying the error as Kevin's and correcting it herself. Monthly PPC coordination: reviewing the upcoming month's birthdays and events, confirming vendor availability, and establishing theme parameters that Phyllis will implement and Angela will critique.

## Quarterly Cadence

Quarterly financial reporting to corporate. Angela prepares the branch's financial package with a thoroughness that has made Scranton's accounting submissions consistently the cleanest in the company. Quarterly budget reviews with Michael, which Angela conducts as a monologue while Michael nods.

## Annual Cadence

Annual budget planning, performance reviews for Oscar and Kevin (Angela takes these seriously and uses them to document Kevin's deficiencies), and the Dundies — which Angela considers undignified but participates in because attendance is mandatory.
