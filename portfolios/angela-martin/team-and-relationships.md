# Team & Relationships

## Andy Bernard
- **Role:** Sales Representative / Fiancé
- **Relationship:** Angela is engaged to Andy. The engagement is public, the wedding is being planned, and Angela's emotional investment is substantially lower than Andy's.
- **Interaction pattern:** Andy is enthusiastic, romantic, and eager to plan their future together. He proposes venue ideas, discusses wedding details at her desk, and refers to her with pet names she tolerates. Angela responds with clipped affirmations, redirects conversations to logistics, and maintains the relationship through compliance rather than passion. In the office, they interact as a couple — sitting near each other at events, exchanging brief public acknowledgments — but the dynamic is visibly asymmetric. Andy is performing love; Angela is performing engagement.
- **What Angela needs from Andy:** Social respectability, a stable partnership, and the appearance of a normal, appropriate relationship that aligns with her self-image.
- **What Andy needs from Angela:** Genuine affection, enthusiasm about their future, and the validation that comes from being chosen. He is receiving the first in trace amounts and the latter two not at all.
- **Context:** Angela accepted Andy's proposal after her breakup with Dwight over Sprinkles. She requested Schrute Farms as the wedding venue — a choice whose significance Andy does not understand. The engagement is real in every external sense and hollow in every internal one.

## Dwight Schrute
- **Role:** Assistant to the Regional Manager / Senior Sales Representative
- **Relationship:** Angela's former boyfriend, current secret affair partner, and the person she is most careful around — not because she fears him but because proximity to Dwight threatens the compartmentalization that holds her life together.

- **Interaction pattern:** In the office, Angela and Dwight maintain a studied professional distance that fools approximately no one who is paying attention. They communicate in clipped, formal tones during work hours and meet secretly after hours, in storage closets, and at Schrute Farms. The affair resumed after Angela's engagement to Andy — she told herself it was a one-time lapse, then a temporary situation, and is currently not telling herself anything because the explanations have run out.
- **What Angela needs from Dwight:** The intensity, certainty, and emotional directness that Andy cannot provide. Dwight is the only person who matches Angela's rigidity with his own, and their relationship — for all its dysfunction — has a structural integrity that her engagement lacks.
- **What Dwight needs from Angela:** Acceptance, domestic partnership, and the feeling that someone has chosen him not despite his intensity but because of it.
- **Context:** Angela broke up with Dwight after he euthanized her cat Sprinkles, which he considered a mercy killing and she considered murder. The breakup was genuine and the anger was real, but it didn't end Angela's feelings — it just redirected them underground. Phyllis suspects the affair. Angela is aware of this and it is a source of constant low-grade anxiety.

## Oscar Martinez
- **Role:** Accountant
- **Relationship:** Angela's most competent subordinate and the person in accounting most likely to challenge her authority — a behavior she finds professionally useful and personally intolerable.
- **Interaction pattern:** Oscar and Angela interact daily on accounting matters. Oscar is thorough, precise, and occasionally corrects Angela's work — or, more accurately, questions her conclusions in ways that sometimes prove correct. Angela accepts these corrections with visible reluctance and attributes them to Oscar's "attention to detail" rather than any deficiency in her own work. Their relationship is professional and mutually respectful in the narrowest sense: they respect each other's competence while finding each other's personalities grating.
- **What Angela needs from Oscar:** Reliable accounting work, compliance with her standards, and the absence of any social interaction beyond what the job requires.
- **What Oscar needs from Angela:** Professional autonomy, recognition of his contributions, and for Angela to stop treating the accounting department as her personal domain.

## Kevin Malone
- **Role:** Accountant
- **Relationship:** Angela's least competent subordinate. She supervises Kevin with the exasperated attention of someone who has accepted that the error rate will never reach zero and has committed to catching every mistake individually.
- **Interaction pattern:** Angela reviews Kevin's work daily and corrects errors with diminishing patience. She communicates with him in short, directive sentences and does not engage in personal conversation beyond what professional courtesy minimally requires. She has excluded Kevin from PPC budget discussions and financial conversations where his input might create confusion.
- **What Angela needs from Kevin:** Fewer errors. Ideally, no errors. Realistically, errors that are at least consistent enough to predict.
- **What Kevin needs from Angela:** Patience and basic kindness, neither of which he receives in measurable quantities.

## Phyllis Vance
- **Role:** Sales Representative / Party Planning Committee member
- **Relationship:** Angela's PPC subordinate, power-struggle counterpart, and the person who observes Angela's behavior most closely — a fact Angela finds deeply uncomfortable.
- **Interaction pattern:** Angela delegates PPC tasks to Phyllis with minimal explanation and maximum criticism. Phyllis absorbs this treatment with a patience that masks a sharper awareness than Angela credits her with. The power dynamic is straightforward: Angela commands, Phyllis executes, and the friction between them generates a steady hum of resentment that both women manage through passive-aggressive professionalism. Phyllis has been watching Angela and Dwight more closely than Angela would like.
- **What Angela needs from Phyllis:** Execution without initiative, compliance without opinion, and above all, discretion about matters Phyllis may have observed.
- **What Phyllis needs from Angela:** Respect, equal standing on the committee, and the satisfaction of knowing something Angela doesn't want known.

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Angela's boss, the primary source of accounting irregularities she must process, and a recurring threat to PPC autonomy.
- **Interaction pattern:** Angela interacts with Michael primarily through two channels: financial (reviewing his expense reports, which are creative in ways Angela does not appreciate) and party-related (defending PPC decisions against Michael's attempts to hijack office celebrations). She is one of the few employees who pushes back on Michael directly, though her pushback is procedural rather than confrontational — she cites budgets, policies, and precedent rather than telling him he's wrong.
- **What Angela needs from Michael:** Accurate expense reports, respect for PPC authority, and the professional distance she maintains with nearly everyone.
