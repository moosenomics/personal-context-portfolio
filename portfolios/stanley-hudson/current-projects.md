# Current Projects

## Existing Account Maintenance
- **What:** Ongoing management of Stanley's long-standing client portfolio — primarily small and mid-size businesses that have been buying paper from him for years.
- **Status:** Stable, as always
- **Stanley's role:** Sole account manager for his book of business
- **Key detail:** Stanley's accounts are the definition of steady-state. Reorders come in on schedule. Clients don't complain because Stanley doesn't give them reason to. He adds virtually no new accounts and loses virtually none. His quarterly numbers are consistently adequate — never the top performer, never the subject of a performance improvement conversation. This is by design.
- **Priority:** The only work priority Stanley recognizes

## Crossword Puzzle Completion
- **What:** Stanley works through crossword puzzle books at a rate of approximately five per year, primarily during meetings, conference calls, and any period when Michael is speaking.
- **Status:** Perpetually active
- **Stanley's role:** Sole contributor
- **Key detail:** This is not a joke entry. Stanley's crossword habit is the scaffolding that holds his patience together. Without it, his tolerance for the daily operations of Dunder Mifflin Scranton would collapse entirely. Michael has attempted to confiscate the puzzle book on multiple occasions. The last attempt produced the "Did I stutter?" incident, and nobody has tried since.
- **Priority:** Higher than anything Michael has ever put on the whiteboard

## Retirement Feasibility Assessment
- **What:** Stanley's private, ongoing calculation of whether he can afford to retire yet.
- **Status:** Active, discouraging
- **Stanley's role:** Financial modeler, pessimist
- **Key detail:** Stanley has been running the numbers on retirement since he was thirty-seven years old. The math never works out the way he wants it to. His daughter's school tuition, his mortgage, and the general cost of living in northeastern Pennsylvania keep the timeline receding. He revisits the spreadsheet every few months, adjusts the assumptions optimistically, stares at the result, and closes the laptop. His dream retirement involves either a decommissioned lighthouse where no one knows he lives, or a porch in Florida. He does not share these calculations with anyone at the office because (a) it's none of their business and (b) Michael would throw him a "Halfway to Retirement" party and Stanley would have the second heart attack.
- **Priority:** The only thing Stanley thinks about after 5 PM that is work-adjacent

## Mystery Stories Backlog
- **What:** Stanley maintains a mental queue of television mystery shows and occasionally mystery novels he intends to get through. This is referenced here because it occupies more of his mental bandwidth during work hours than any actual project.
- **Status:** Always growing, never fully current
- **Stanley's role:** Viewer, critic
- **Key detail:** Stanley's evening routine involves red wine and mystery stories. He is privately opinionated about plot construction and has strong views on which shows respect the audience's intelligence. He will not discuss this at work because it would invite conversation.
- **Priority:** Personal priority only, but it outranks everything on this list except retirement
