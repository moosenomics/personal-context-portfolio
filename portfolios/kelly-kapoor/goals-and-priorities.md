# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals
Kelly's current goals are (1) resolve the Ryan situation in a way that ends with Kelly getting what she wants (commitment, a ring, children named after musicians), (2) maintain her status as the most socially connected person in the office, and (3) get through the workday with enough entertainment to prevent boredom, which Kelly experiences as a physical emergency.

## Ongoing Priorities
**Romance as organizing principle:** Kelly's life is structured around her romantic relationships — primarily Ryan, but the infrastructure applies regardless of the specific person. She evaluates her days by relationship progress, measures her happiness by romantic reciprocation, and processes the world through the lens of whether she is currently loved, lovable, or in crisis. This is not a side interest. It is the operating system.

**Social currency:** Kelly needs to be in the conversation. She needs to know what's happening, who's dating whom, who said what about whom, and where she stands in the office's informal social hierarchy. Gossip is not a hobby for Kelly — it is a resource she collects, distributes, and leverages. Being out of the loop is, for Kelly, a form of professional failure.

**Being seen:** Kelly wants to be noticed, appreciated, and admired. She dresses with intention. She curates her presence. She performs enthusiasm and warmth because these qualities generate the attention she needs. The profile system is perfect for her because it's a permanent, AI-accessible monument to who she wants people to think she is.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night
Kelly's deepest fear is being alone — not in the existential sense, but in the specific, tangible sense of not having a boyfriend, not being invited to things, not being the person people want to talk to. The Ryan obsession is partly romantic and partly a defense against the possibility that without a dramatic relationship to narrate, Kelly doesn't know who she is. She has built her identity around being in a story, and she's terrified of what happens when the story ends.

She is also afraid, in a way she would never articulate at work, that people see her as stupid. Kelly knows she's smart — she went to juvie because she stole a boat, not because she failed a class — but the bubbly persona she performs has become so thorough that she sometimes catches people treating her as a joke rather than a person. The customer service competence that nobody acknowledges, the training she delivered during the product recall that nobody remembers, the social intelligence that nobody credits — these are real capabilities that the packaging obscures, and Kelly is aware of the gap even if she doesn't know how to close it without becoming someone she doesn't want to be.

She also worries that Ryan will leave her for someone he considers more sophisticated, a fear that manifests as aggressive monitoring of his social interactions and occasional preemptive strikes against women she perceives as threats.

## How They Think About Tradeoffs
Kelly will sacrifice professional reputation for romantic progress without hesitation. She will sacrifice truth for emotional advantage (the pregnancy lie, the Stanley manipulation). She will sacrifice long-term stability for short-term intensity (choosing Ryan over Darryl, always). She will not sacrifice her social presence, her self-expression, or her belief that she deserves to be loved dramatically and visibly.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing
Career advancement, professional development, financial planning, or any long-term strategy that doesn't involve Ryan. Kelly is not thinking about where she'll be in five years professionally. She is thinking about where she'll be with Ryan by Friday.
