# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools
Kelly works from a standard Dell desktop in the annex with a headset for customer calls, Outlook for email, and the Dunder Mifflin customer service logging system. Her phone — the personal one, not the desk phone — is her most-used tool by a wide margin. She texts, browses celebrity news, checks Ryan's social media, and scrolls through fashion content between customer calls with the fluidity of someone who treats multitasking as a lifestyle rather than a skill.

Kelly has strong opinions about her headset (it messes up her hair) and her computer (it's slow and she deserves a better one). She uses Netflix at her desk when call volume is low, which is more often than the branch's customer satisfaction scores would suggest.

Her personal context portfolio is served via MCP to her AI assistant. Kelly approached the profile system the way she approaches everything: with maximum volume and minimal self-editing. She wrote extensively, updated frequently, and has used her AI assistant for drafting text messages to Ryan, planning outfits, researching celebrity gossip that she could have found herself faster by scrolling, and asking questions about other people's profiles that her AI faithfully answers. Kelly is probably the heaviest user of the profile system in the office — not because she's the most strategic, but because she treats it like a social media platform that happens to be connected to work.

## Key Workflows
Customer calls arrive on her desk phone, Kelly answers, resolves or escalates, and logs the interaction. Between calls, everything else happens — the socializing, the texting, the walks to the main office, the commentary on everyone else's lives. Internal communication happens face-to-face whenever possible, because Kelly prefers talking to typing, and she prefers talking to not talking even more.

## Current System Issues
Kelly has no complaints about the profile system. She loves it. She loves that her AI knows about her and can help her draft messages in her voice. She loves reading other people's profiles. She noticed that Dwight's profile mentions dolphin psychology and interpretive dance, and she told three people about it within the hour. She has not considered whether this information is accurate or how it got there, because Kelly does not think about systems — she thinks about content.
