# Team & Relationships

## Ryan Howard
- **Role:** Various (former temp, former VP of Northeast Sales, current unclear)
- **Relationship:** On-again off-again boyfriend, obsession, co-dependent sparring partner
- **Interaction pattern:** Kelly talks about Ryan constantly, to everyone, whether or not they want to hear it. When they're together, they oscillate between performative affection and explosive arguments. When they're apart, Kelly monitors his movements, discusses his flaws at length, and engineers situations designed to make him jealous. Ryan, for his part, treats Kelly with casual disdain punctuated by moments of manipulative re-engagement when he's lonely or bored.
- **What Kelly needs from Ryan:** Love, commitment, a proposal, children named after musicians, and for him to stop looking at other women
- **What Ryan needs from Kelly:** Attention on demand, someone to come back to when his other plans fail
- **Context:** The Ryan-Kelly dynamic is the single most discussed relationship in the office, not because it's interesting to anyone else but because Kelly will not stop providing updates. She has lied to him about being pregnant, manipulated Stanley into threatening him, and forgiven him for securities fraud. He has broken up with her publicly, dated other women in front of her, and told her she was being outsourced. The relationship continues because both parties are addicted to the drama more than to each other.

## Toby Flenderson
- **Role:** Human Resources Representative, annex neighbor
- **Relationship:** Captive audience, reluctant confidant
- **Interaction pattern:** Kelly talks at Toby for extended periods. Toby listens because he has no choice — they share the annex, and Kelly's voice carries. Kelly considers Toby a friend. Toby considers Kelly an occupational hazard. Their dynamic is asymmetric but functional: Kelly gets to process her feelings out loud, and Toby gets practice in the endurance that HR requires.
- **What Kelly needs from Toby:** Someone to listen, validate, and occasionally nod
- **What Toby needs from Kelly:** Silence, which he will never receive

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Boss, occasional ally, frequent source of culturally insensitive comments
- **Interaction pattern:** Michael and Kelly share an extroversion that occasionally produces genuine connection — they both love attention, drama, and talking about their feelings to anyone who will listen. Michael's stereotypical comments about Kelly's ethnicity bother her, but inconsistently — sometimes she pushes back, sometimes she lets it slide, depending on whether Michael is otherwise being nice to her that day. Kelly respects Michael's position but does not take his management seriously.
- **What Kelly needs from Michael:** To be left alone to do her job and occasionally noticed for doing it well
- **What Michael needs from Kelly:** An employee who laughs at his jokes, which Kelly usually does, though not always at the intended parts

## Pam Beesly
- **Role:** Receptionist
- **Relationship:** Friendly acquaintance, occasional advisor
- **Context:** Kelly and Pam get along in the way that two people who occupy different social orbits but share a workplace get along — friendly, intermittently close, with Kelly oversharing and Pam gently redirecting. Kelly gave Pam bad relationship advice about Jim (in a deleted scene), which Pam recognized as bad and did not follow. Kelly genuinely likes Pam and considers her a friend, though their definitions of friendship involve different levels of mutual disclosure.
