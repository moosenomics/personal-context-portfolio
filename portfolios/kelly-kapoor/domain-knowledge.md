# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise
**Customer service and de-escalation:** Kelly is genuinely skilled at managing angry customers. She can read tone, match emotional energy, pivot from validation to resolution, and leave callers feeling heard — all while sitting in the annex in a pink cardigan. During the product recall crisis, she trained three accountants on phone technique in a single afternoon, demonstrating that she understands her craft well enough to teach it. She has developed scripts and approaches for different caller types: the angry screamer (let them vent, then apologize), the passive-aggressive emailer (call them directly, be warm), the serial complainer (acknowledge the pattern, offer a concrete fix). This expertise is invisible to the office because nobody visits the annex to watch Kelly work.

**Pop culture, celebrity news, and entertainment media:** Kelly's knowledge of current celebrities, music, television, film, and fashion is encyclopedic and constantly updated. She can identify trends, predict red carpet choices, and provide detailed analysis of reality TV relationships with a fluency that, applied to any other domain, would be considered expertise. She processes information about pop culture faster than she processes anything else, and her retention is remarkable.

**Social dynamics and interpersonal manipulation:** Kelly understands people — their insecurities, their desires, their triggers — at an intuitive level. She lied to Stanley about Ryan flirting with his daughter because she knew exactly how Stanley would react. She performs vulnerability to generate sympathy. She deploys tears strategically. She told Ryan she was pregnant because she correctly predicted it would prevent a breakup, even if temporarily. These are manipulative behaviors, but they demonstrate a sophisticated model of human psychology that Kelly applies instinctively.

**Fashion and personal presentation:** Kelly curates her appearance with intention and knowledge. She follows trends, understands color theory as applied to outfits, and has opinions about her coworkers' clothing choices that are specific enough to fill a column. She takes personal presentation seriously and judges others by how seriously they take it.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner
**Financial anything:** Kelly does not understand her own finances, much less corporate budgets. She once asked Oscar what a "surplus" was and then lost interest before he finished the first sentence. Numbers in general are not her domain.

**Technology beyond social media:** Kelly uses her phone expertly for social purposes but has limited fluency with office technology, data management, or system architecture. She uses the profile system enthusiastically but has no understanding of how it works beneath the surface.

**Long-term planning:** Kelly operates in the immediate present. She does not plan for next month, next year, or retirement. She does not have a career strategy. Her decision-making horizon extends approximately to the end of the current emotional crisis, which is rarely more than a week away.

**Self-regulation:** Kelly's emotional responses are intense and immediate. She has limited ability to modulate her reactions to match the severity of the situation, which produces tears over birthday cakes and rage over unreturned texts. This is not a knowledge gap in the traditional sense, but it functions as one — Kelly doesn't yet have the tools to separate what she feels from how she responds.

## Frameworks / Mental Models
Kelly processes the world through a **romantic narrative framework** — she is always the protagonist of a love story, and every event is evaluated by its contribution to or detraction from the romantic plot. Work is the setting, not the story. Coworkers are supporting characters. Ryan is the love interest who keeps writing himself out of the script and then back in.

She also operates from a **social visibility model** — if something isn't seen, shared, or discussed, it barely exists. This explains the profile over-sharing, the gossip distribution, and the discomfort with solitude. Kelly's reality is constructed through social validation, and she maintains it by keeping herself at the center of as many conversations as possible.
