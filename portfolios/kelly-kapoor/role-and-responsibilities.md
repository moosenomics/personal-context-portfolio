# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Customer Service Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott (Regional Manager)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities
Kelly is the sole point of contact for all customer complaints at the Scranton branch. She answers inbound calls from clients with issues — shipping delays, billing errors, quality problems, order discrepancies — and resolves them through a combination of empathy, apology, and escalation when necessary. She maintains the customer service log, tracks complaint resolution, and provides feedback to the sales team on recurring client issues. During the product recall crisis (the watermark incident), Kelly single-handedly managed the initial complaint volume and then trained Oscar, Angela, and Kevin to handle overflow calls — demonstrating that she is, when the situation demands it, a capable trainer and process manager.

## Reporting Structure
Reports to Michael Scott, who rarely interacts with Kelly in her professional capacity because customer service is the one department at Scranton that functions without his involvement. Kelly operates from the annex — a back area physically separated from the main office — which means she has less day-to-day visibility with management than the sales team. She shares the annex with Toby Flenderson (HR), who provides a semi-willing audience for her personal commentary and an unwilling sounding board for her relationship problems.

## Typical Week
Kelly arrives at her desk in the annex and begins her day by checking voicemail, reviewing any overnight complaints, and talking to Toby about whatever happened on television last night, in her personal life, or in the lives of celebrities she follows — often all three simultaneously. Customer calls come throughout the day, and Kelly handles them with genuine warmth and practiced skill. Between calls, she walks into the main office to socialize, gossip, check on Ryan, or insert herself into whatever conversation is happening. She spends time on her phone texting, browsing celebrity news, and monitoring Ryan's social media presence. Lunch is social — she eats with whoever will listen, which varies depending on the day's gossip market.

Kelly's workflow is reactive by nature — customer calls come when they come, and Kelly handles them. The gaps between calls are filled with the personal and social activities that define her presence in the office. She leaves at 5 PM, sometimes earlier if things are slow and nobody's watching, sometimes later if she's in the middle of a story.
