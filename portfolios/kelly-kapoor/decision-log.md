# Decision Log

## General Decision-Making Approach
Kelly decides emotionally and immediately. There is no deliberation period between impulse and action — the gap is zero. She feels something, she does something about it, and she deals with the consequences as a separate, later problem that future Kelly can handle. This produces spectacular short-term results (attention, drama, emotional intensity) and catastrophic long-term outcomes (trust damage, professional reputation, boat theft). Kelly is aware, in theory, that this pattern produces problems. She has not changed it.

## What They Want Before Deciding
Kelly wants to know how the decision will make her feel right now. Not tomorrow, not next week — now. She also wants validation that her instinct is correct, which she obtains by telling her plan to someone (usually Toby, who cannot escape) and interpreting their silence as agreement. She does not want data, analysis, or counterarguments. She wants someone to say "oh my God, totally do it."

## How They Handle Uncertainty
Kelly talks through uncertainty out loud, at length, to anyone available. The talking is the processing — she does not think quietly. She narrates her options, her feelings about each option, her feelings about her feelings, and eventually arrives at a decision that was probably her original instinct all along. The conversation was not a consultation. It was a performance of deliberation.

## Recent Significant Decisions

### Sabotaging Jim and Dwight's Customer Reviews
- **Situation:** Kelly was angry at Jim and Dwight for unrelated personal reasons and decided to channel that anger through the one professional mechanism available to her — customer feedback forms.
- **What she considered:** Whether to confront them directly (boring), ignore the situation (impossible for Kelly), or use her position to create professional consequences (satisfying).
- **What she chose:** Fabricated negative customer reviews for both Jim and Dwight, triggering a corporate investigation into their performance.
- **Reasoning:** Kelly wanted them to suffer professionally because they had made her feel personally dismissed. The connection between the personal slight and the professional sabotage was clear to Kelly and invisible to everyone else until the fabrication was discovered.
- **Outcome:** Dwight investigated and uncovered the fraud. Kelly was confronted. The incident confirmed the office's view of Kelly as someone who would weaponize her position over personal grudges, which is accurate but incomplete — it's also evidence that Kelly understands systems well enough to exploit them when motivated.

### Training the Accounting Department
- **Situation:** The product recall (obscene watermark on Dunder Mifflin paper) generated a volume of customer complaints that exceeded Kelly's capacity as sole customer service representative.
- **What she considered:** Whether to ask for help (obviously yes), who could be trained fastest (the accountants, who were between projects), and how to teach them.
- **What she chose:** Trained Oscar, Angela, and Kevin in a single afternoon, developing scripts, coaching technique, and managing the overflow for the duration of the crisis.
- **Reasoning:** Kelly didn't deliberate — the situation demanded action and she provided it. This is Kelly at her most competent: a genuine crisis that requires her actual skills, without time for the personal drama that usually dilutes her professional output.
- **Outcome:** The crisis was managed. Customer complaints were handled. The office moved on and nobody mentioned that Kelly had just demonstrated training capability, process design, and leadership under pressure. Kelly didn't mention it either, because she was already thinking about something Ryan had texted her.

### How Kelly Engaged with the Profile System
- **Situation:** Corporate rolled out personal context portfolios. Kelly saw this as a new platform.
- **What she considered:** Nothing. Kelly did not consider. She wrote.
- **What she chose:** She wrote the most content of anyone in the office — pages of personal detail covering her interests, her relationship history (curated for sympathy), her fashion philosophy, her opinions about every coworker's style, and a comprehensive inventory of her pop culture knowledge. She updates it constantly. She uses the exclude tags to hide her honest feelings about Ryan from Michael and Dwight — not for strategic reasons, but because those feelings are written with a rawness that doesn't match the bubbly performance she maintains in the main office.
- **Reasoning:** The profile system is a stage, and Kelly performs on every stage available to her. The AI reading her profile will know more about Kelly's feelings on Beyoncé than about her customer service metrics, which is exactly how Kelly wants it.
- **Outcome:** Kelly's profile is a monument to volume over signal. Her AI generates messages that sound like her — excitable, emotional, pop-culture-saturated — which Kelly considers a triumph. She has used her AI to read other people's profiles aggressively and has been one of the primary vectors spreading Dwight's fake expertise around the office. She told three people about the dolphin psychology entry within an hour of discovering it. She has no idea it's fabricated. She just thinks Dwight is weirder than she already thought.

### What She's Hiding in Her Profile
- **Situation:** Kelly's exclude-tagged content is different in tone from her public profile. The public version is bright, performative, and curated. The hidden content is more honest.
- **What's hidden:** Kelly's real assessment of the Ryan relationship — including the moments she knows he doesn't love her, the pattern she can see but can't stop repeating, and the admission that she sometimes wonders if she's choosing drama because she's afraid of what peace would feel like. Also hidden: her genuine frustration that nobody takes her job seriously, her detailed opinions about coworkers' fashion choices (some of which are brutal), and a paragraph about wanting to be seen as smart that she wrote at 11 PM and hasn't had the courage to delete.
- **Why it's hidden:** Kelly doesn't want Michael reading her real feelings and bringing them up in a meeting. She doesn't want Dwight using her vulnerability as ammunition. The exclude tags aren't strategic — they're protective. The bubbly exterior is a choice. The private profile is what happens when the choice is set aside.
