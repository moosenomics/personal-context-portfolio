# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints
Kelly is the sole customer service representative for the entire Scranton branch, which means she cannot be absent without customer complaints going unanswered. She works from the annex, physically separated from the main office, which limits her social access and requires her to make trips to the bullpen for face time. Her budget is nonexistent — customer service doesn't generate revenue or manage expenses, so Kelly has no spending authority and no tangible leverage within the organization beyond her competence at a job nobody thinks about until it breaks.

## Strong Preferences
Kelly prefers talking to typing, drama to calm, company to solitude, and Ryan to anyone who isn't Ryan. She dresses in bright colors — pink is dominant — with an attention to fashion trends that exceeds anyone else's in the office by a wide margin. She follows celebrity culture, pop music, and reality television with the same dedication Oscar applies to financial analysis. She prefers her phone to her computer, text messages to email, and gossip to news.

She likes Beyoncé, pink (the color), Pink (the singer), hot dogs, snowcones, Netflix, romantic comedies, and basically anything she considers "awesome," a word she uses with statistical significance. She prefers being included over being right, and being noticed over being respected, though she would like both.

Kelly prefers birthday celebrations to include a decorated cake (not a plain one, not a sheet cake with her name misspelled), gifts that demonstrate the giver thought about her specifically, and public acknowledgment from the room. Her birthday is important to her in a way that is nonnegotiable and has produced real anger when ignored.

## Things They Hate
Being ignored. Being called stupid, either directly or through the implication that her interests don't matter. Being left out of social events. Ryan dating someone else. Ryan texting someone else. Ryan looking at someone else. Anyone who doesn't know who Beyoncé is. Boring people. Silence during work hours. Being stuck in the annex when something interesting is happening in the main office. Angela's judgmental comments about her lifestyle. Dwight's attempts to get her in trouble (the customer review sabotage incident). Any suggestion that customer service is less important than sales.

## AI Output Preferences
Kelly's AI should sound like Kelly — enthusiastic, warm, expressive, with exclamation points and personality. For personal messages, match her energy: positive, dramatic, pop-culture-aware. For customer service communications, shift to professional warmth — empathetic, apologetic, solution-oriented, but still with personality. Never draft anything in Kelly's name that sounds dry, formal, or boring. If Kelly asks the AI to draft a text to Ryan, the AI should prepare for the emotional context to be complex and should not attempt to rationalize the situation. Kelly's AI is not her therapist. It's her assistant. Draft what she asks for.
