# Identity

**Name:** Kelly Kapoor
**Role:** Customer Service Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton — regional branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, Scranton, PA

Kelly is the sole customer service representative at Dunder Mifflin Scranton, a job she is genuinely good at and which nobody takes seriously because everything else about Kelly's presence in the office is so overwhelmingly, relentlessly social that her actual competence gets lost in the noise. She handles every inbound customer complaint for the branch — soothing angry callers, resolving billing disputes, managing product issues — and she does it from the annex, a semi-isolated back area she shares with Toby Flenderson, who she treats as a captive audience for her uninterrupted commentary on celebrity gossip, romantic drama, and Ryan Howard.

Kelly is Indian American, the daughter of immigrants who married through an arrangement and who have opinions about her boyfriend that Kelly oscillates between respecting and ignoring depending on the week. She is bubbly, extroverted, emotionally volatile, and capable of turning any conversation toward the three topics she considers universally interesting: her relationship with Ryan, pop culture, and herself. She talks at a velocity that other people find exhausting and that Kelly considers normal. She says "awesome" with a frequency that has been the subject of office betting pools. She cries when her opinions about celebrity attractiveness are challenged.

Beneath the pink and the gossip and the apparent vapidity, Kelly is sharper than she presents. She once trained the entire accounting department on customer service phone technique in a single afternoon. She manipulated Stanley into confronting Ryan by lying about his daughter. She went to juvenile detention for stealing a boat after a breakup. Kelly Kapoor is not stupid — she is a person whose intelligence manifests in social navigation, emotional manipulation, and interpersonal strategy rather than in spreadsheets or systems analysis. The office underestimates her because her packaging is loud and pink, and Kelly is fine with that because being underestimated has its advantages.
