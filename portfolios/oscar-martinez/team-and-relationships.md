# Team & Relationships

## Angela Martin
- **Role:** Senior Accountant, Head of Party Planning Committee (contested)
- **Relationship:** Direct colleague, ideological opposite, reluctant collaborator
- **Interaction pattern:** Oscar and Angela share a workspace and review each other's work daily. Their interactions are professional and clipped. Angela's conservative Christian views and Oscar's existence as an openly gay man create a persistent tension that both parties manage through mutual avoidance of personal topics. Angela initially reacted to Oscar's outing with overt hostility, later apologized, and has since settled into a pattern of grudging professional respect punctuated by occasional coldness.
- **What Oscar needs from Angela:** Competent work review and the absence of moralizing commentary
- **What Angela needs from Oscar:** Accurate financial work that she doesn't have to redo
- **Context:** Oscar is aware of Angela's affair with Dwight — the entire office is, after Phyllis's revelation. Oscar finds Angela's hypocrisy on matters of personal conduct particularly grating given the moral judgments she has directed at him. He does not confront her about this because he considers it beneath him, but the awareness informs every interaction.

## Kevin Malone
- **Role:** Accountant
- **Relationship:** Friend, source of both affection and exasperation
- **Interaction pattern:** Oscar and Kevin share a genuine friendship that operates on a dynamic of intellectual asymmetry. Kevin asks questions that reveal gaps in understanding Oscar finds alarming for a professional accountant. Oscar answers them patiently, corrects Kevin's errors, and occasionally plays Hateball (paper football) with him across Angela's desk, which Angela hates. Kevin's warmth and lack of pretension are qualities Oscar values even as Kevin's professional competence concerns him.
- **What Oscar needs from Kevin:** Companionship, data entry assistance, and the reassurance that someone in accounting is having a good time
- **What Kevin needs from Oscar:** Patient explanation, error correction, and someone who treats him as a person rather than an impediment
- **Context:** The Oscar-Kevin dynamic is the emotional core of the accounting department. Angela provides structure and standards. Kevin provides humanity and errors. Oscar provides competence and barely-contained sighs. The three of them function as a unit, improbably.

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Boss, source of chronic intellectual frustration and occasional inadvertent harm
- **Interaction pattern:** Michael comes to Oscar with financial questions he should already understand, cultural questions that reveal ignorance Oscar must navigate delicately, and management decisions that Oscar can see are financially unsound but cannot override. Oscar explains, corrects, and clarifies with diminishing patience. Michael listens with selective comprehension and credits himself for Oscar's insights.
- **What Oscar needs from Michael:** Basic competence, or failing that, the willingness to defer to people who have it
- **What Michael needs from Oscar:** Someone to make the numbers make sense and to validate decisions Michael has already made
- **Context:** Michael outed Oscar to the entire office in the most public and humiliating way possible. Oscar has moved past the incident — or more precisely, around it — but it established a permanent floor beneath which his regard for Michael cannot sink further. He feels a mixture of amusement, pity, and frustration toward Michael that he has described as "complicated" and which is better described as exhausting.

## Pam Beesly
- **Role:** Receptionist, Finer Things Club co-founder
- **Relationship:** Intellectual ally, cultural companion
- **Interaction pattern:** Oscar and Pam interact primarily through the Finer Things Club, where they discuss literature and art with an earnestness that neither of them exhibits elsewhere in the office. Pam brings warmth and intuition to the discussions; Oscar brings analytical rigor and strong opinions about narrative structure. They are genuinely fond of each other in a way that doesn't require constant maintenance.
- **What Oscar needs from Pam:** Intellectual companionship, someone who reads the assigned book
- **What Pam needs from Oscar:** The same, plus validation that it's okay to want more than the office provides

## Toby Flenderson
- **Role:** Human Resources Representative, Finer Things Club co-founder
- **Relationship:** Ally of convenience, cultural companion
- **Context:** Oscar and Toby share membership in both the Finer Things Club and the Coalition for Reason. They are two of the most clear-eyed people in the office, which means they share the burden of seeing things as they are while everyone else operates in various states of denial. Toby's HR investigation into the profile tampering is something Oscar suspects exists but hasn't confirmed, because Toby doesn't share active investigations and Oscar respects process even when he's frustrated by it.
