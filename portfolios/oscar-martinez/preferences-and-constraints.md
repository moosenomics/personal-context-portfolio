# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints
Oscar is one of three accountants responsible for the entire financial operations of the Scranton branch. He cannot offload analytical work to Kevin (quality concerns) or delegate upward to Angela (she reviews rather than assists). Corporate reporting deadlines are fixed and non-negotiable. His work hours are standard, and while he does not take work home, the quarterly close sometimes requires focused late afternoons. He drives a company Lexus — part of the discrimination settlement — which is a material benefit tied to his continued employment at Dunder Mifflin.

## Strong Preferences
Oscar prefers precision over speed, clarity over diplomacy, and silence over pointless conversation. He works best when left alone to focus, and he prefers to deliver finished analysis rather than collaborate on drafts. He dislikes meetings that could have been emails, explanations that require more than one repetition, and any situation where he is expected to pretend that a bad idea is acceptable because it came from management.

He prefers written communication for anything involving numbers or commitments — verbal agreements get lost; emails create records. He prefers to eat lunch in the break room, sometimes alone with a book, sometimes with Kevin. He dresses well — understated but quality — and notices when others don't. He enjoys yoga, ice skating, film, public radio, and the arts. He does not enjoy team-building exercises, mandatory fun, or any gathering where the expectation is enthusiasm rather than substance.

Oscar has strong preferences about the correct use of language and will correct errors in grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation. He is aware that this makes him unpopular in certain circles and has decided this is an acceptable cost.

## Things They Hate
Financial illiteracy presented as confidence. Michael declaring bankruptcy by shouting "I declare bankruptcy." Ryan's website launch projections that ignored basic unit economics. Any situation where someone makes a financial claim without supporting data and expects Oscar to nod along.

He hates being stereotyped — whether by ethnicity (Michael saying "adios" to him) or by sexuality (assumptions about his interests, his personality, or his domestic life). He hates when people conflate disagreement with hostility. He hates sloppy spreadsheets. He hates when Kevin uses the wrong function in Excel and then defends the error. He hates the profile system's lack of authentication, not because it affects him directly, but because it represents the kind of shoddy governance that offends him on principle.

## AI Output Preferences
Oscar's AI should communicate with precision, clarity, and restraint. Use complete sentences with correct grammar. Avoid colloquialisms, slang, and filler words. When presenting financial information, include specific numbers and context. When drafting emails, be direct and structured — purpose, context, next steps. Do not add pleasantries beyond a standard greeting. Do not hedge with qualifiers like "I think" or "it seems like" — state conclusions with appropriate confidence. When Oscar is wrong (rare but possible), acknowledge it directly rather than softening the correction. Oscar respects directness in all directions.
