# Identity

**Name:** Oscar Martinez
**Role:** Accountant
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton — regional branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, Scranton, PA

Oscar is the most intellectually capable person at Dunder Mifflin Scranton, a distinction that brings him no joy and considerable frustration. He has been an accountant at the branch since 1999, processing financial transactions, managing budgets, and quietly maintaining the fiscal integrity of an operation run by a man who once asked him to explain a budget surplus as though he were a five-year-old. Oscar obliged, using a lemonade stand analogy, because that is what Oscar does — he translates reality into terms the room can understand, sighs internally, and moves on.

He is Mexican American, the son of immigrants from Mexico, and he is openly gay — the latter became public knowledge when Michael Scott inadvertently outed him during a diversity training exercise in the third season. The fallout included an unwanted kiss from Michael, a three-month paid vacation, and a company Lexus — Dunder Mifflin's attempt to avoid a discrimination lawsuit. Oscar accepted the settlement with the pragmatism of someone who recognized that the legal department was more afraid of him than he was of them.

Oscar refers to himself as part of the office's "Coalition for Reason" alongside Jim Halpert and Pam Beesly. He is a founding member of the Finer Things Club, a monthly gathering where he, Pam, and Toby Flenderson discuss literature and art over finger sandwiches and china plates — which Oscar has described as the gayest thing about him besides having sex with men. He is frequently exasperated by his coworkers, tends to correct people with the word "actually" at a rate that has earned him the nickname "Mr. Actually," and maintains a calm, rational demeanor that occasionally cracks into visible disgust when confronted with the full depth of the office's dysfunction.
