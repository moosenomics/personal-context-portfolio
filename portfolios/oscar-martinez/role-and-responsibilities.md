# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Accountant
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott (Regional Manager), Angela Martin (Senior Accountant, nominally)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities
Oscar processes the financial transactions generated by the sales team, tracks and updates inventory data, reconciles accounts receivable and payable with client accounting departments, and maintains the branch's budgetary records. He handles expense reports, audits petty cash, prepares financial summaries for corporate, and serves as the de facto financial advisor to anyone in the office who asks — including Michael, who asks regularly and understands intermittently. Oscar also flags budgetary anomalies, identifies surpluses and shortfalls, and explains the implications of financial decisions to people who would prefer not to hear them.

## Reporting Structure
Oscar reports to Michael Scott on the org chart and to Angela Martin within the accounting department's internal hierarchy. Angela is Senior Accountant and technically leads the three-person accounting team (Angela, Oscar, Kevin Malone). In practice, Oscar does the most rigorous work, Angela maintains the strictest standards, and Kevin provides moral support. Oscar and Angela's working relationship is professional but strained by personal and ideological differences that predate the profile system and will outlast it.

## Typical Week
Oscar arrives on time, settles into the accounting cluster, and begins working through whatever financial processing the sales team generated the previous day. Mornings involve transaction entry, reconciliation, and responding to internal queries from salespeople who don't understand their commission statements. Late mornings frequently bring interruptions — Michael's conferences, Kevin's questions, or Angela's corrections to work Oscar already did correctly. Oscar handles these with diminishing patience as the week progresses.

Afternoons are for deeper analytical work — budget reviews, corporate reporting, and the occasional forensic dive into expense anomalies. Oscar takes his lunch in the break room, sometimes with Kevin, sometimes reading alone. Once a month, the Finer Things Club meets during lunch in the break room to discuss a selected book or cultural topic over cloth napkins and real flatware.

Oscar leaves at 5 PM. He does not bring work home, not because the work doesn't follow him mentally, but because he has drawn a hard line between the office and the life he has constructed outside of it — a life that involves yoga, ice skating, film, literature, and conversations with people who understand compound interest without an analogy.

## Current Extra Workstreams
Founding member of the Finer Things Club. Occasional financial counselor to Michael (unpaid, unappreciated). Informal voice of fiscal reason during any corporate initiative that requires budget justification.
