# Current Projects

## Quarterly Budget Reconciliation
- **What:** Standard end-of-quarter financial close — reconciling all branch accounts, verifying expense reports, preparing summary documents for corporate.
- **Status:** Active, on schedule
- **Oscar's role:** Primary analyst. Angela reviews his work; Kevin assists with data entry when supervised.
- **Key detail:** Oscar handles the analytical heavy lifting of the accounting department. The quarterly close is the most demanding recurring workload, and Oscar approaches it with the thoroughness of someone who knows that any error he misses will be attributed to incompetence rather than the systemic dysfunction that makes errors likely.
- **Priority:** High — this is the core deliverable of Oscar's job.

- **Status:** Active, meeting monthly
- **Oscar's role:** Co-founder, intellectual anchor, selector of readings
- **Key collaborators:** Pam Beesly, Toby Flenderson. Jim Halpert was briefly admitted but did not take the readings seriously.
- **Key detail:** The Finer Things Club is the only voluntary activity at Dunder Mifflin that Oscar genuinely values. It is a small island of intellectual engagement in an ocean of conference room meetings about nothing. Andy Bernard has lobbied for membership and been denied. Oscar considers the club's exclusivity essential to its function — opening it to the general office population would defeat its purpose entirely.
- **Priority:** Personal priority, high. Oscar would be reluctant to admit how much the club means to him.

## Profile System Security Analysis
- **What:** Oscar's independent investigation into the authentication architecture of the personal context portfolio system, prompted by anomalies he's observed in profile data.
- **Status:** Active, informal
- **Oscar's role:** Self-appointed auditor
- **Key detail:** Oscar noticed the same staging exploit that Dwight is using — the JSON update endpoint doesn't verify authorship. He identified this independently, through the kind of systematic curiosity that makes him examine how systems work rather than just using them. He has documented the vulnerability in a personal file, including the authentication gap, the lack of input validation on the staging endpoint, and the absence of any audit log that tracks who submitted profile edits.

Oscar has also noticed that Michael's profile contains a succession planning project Michael has never discussed, and that Dwight's domain-knowledge section includes entries (French Impressionist art criticism, marine mammal behavioral psychology) that are obviously fabricated. He has connected these anomalies to the staging exploit — someone is editing profiles they don't own.

He has not reported this to Michael because Michael would not understand the technical explanation. He has considered telling Toby, who he suspects may already be investigating. He has not told Jim because Jim would find it amusing rather than concerning. Oscar is frustrated that the most obvious governance failure in the system is being treated as entertainment by the people who should be alarmed by it.
- **Priority:** Moderate — Oscar cares about this on principle but hasn't found a productive channel for the information.

## Financial Literacy as Coping Mechanism
- **What:** Oscar's ongoing habit of analyzing the financial decisions of his coworkers, the branch, and the company — not because anyone asks, but because poor financial reasoning causes him physical discomfort.
- **Status:** Perpetual
- **Oscar's role:** Unpaid consultant to anyone who will listen, and several who won't
- **Key detail:** Oscar helped Michael navigate personal bankruptcy after Michael followed Creed's advice to "just declare it." He explained the budget surplus to Michael using a lemonade stand. He identified DM's structural problems before the shareholder meeting. None of this was in his job description. All of it was driven by an inability to watch someone be wrong about numbers without intervening.
- **Priority:** Compulsive rather than strategic
