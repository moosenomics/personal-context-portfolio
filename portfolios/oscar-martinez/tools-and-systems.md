# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools
Oscar works on a standard Dell desktop running the Dunder Mifflin accounting suite, Microsoft Excel, and Outlook. He is the most technically proficient person in accounting — possibly in the office — and uses Excel with a fluency that makes his spreadsheets look simple when they are actually sophisticated. He builds formulas, creates pivot tables, and structures workbooks with the care of someone who understands that one error in row forty-seven cascades through everything below it. His desk phone handles the occasional client call related to invoicing or accounts receivable, but most of Oscar's work is screen-based.

Oscar carries a cell phone and uses it frequently — often on personal calls during work hours, a habit the show has documented since season one. He drives a Lexus RX 400h, a company car provided as part of Dunder Mifflin's settlement after the outing incident.

His personal context portfolio is served via MCP to his AI assistant. Oscar engaged with the system thoughtfully — he wrote a complete, accurate profile because he understands that the output quality of any AI system depends on input quality. He then did what nobody else in the office thought to do: he examined the system's architecture. He tested the update endpoint, reviewed the staging process, and identified the authentication gap that allows unauthorized profile edits. His AI assistant is well-calibrated because his profile is well-written, and he uses it primarily for financial analysis assistance, email drafting, and researching topics for the Finer Things Club.

## Key Workflows
Accounting work follows a daily cycle: process transactions, reconcile accounts, review expense reports, prepare summaries. Oscar works methodically from a task list he maintains in a personal spreadsheet (not the CRM, which he considers poorly designed for accounting workflows). Internal queries come through email or in-person interruptions; Oscar handles them with efficiency and mild irritation depending on the asker. Corporate reporting follows quarterly deadlines that Oscar manages proactively — he prepares early and builds in buffer time for the inevitable complications Kevin or Michael introduce.

## Current System Issues
The profile system has a critical authentication vulnerability that Oscar has documented. The JSON staging endpoint accepts profile updates without verifying that the submitter is the profile owner. There is no audit trail. There is no approval workflow. The system essentially trusts that everyone will only edit their own profile, which, in an office containing Dwight Schrute and Jim Halpert, is an assumption that should never have survived a design review. Oscar has written up the technical details in a personal document but has not found an effective reporting channel — Toby would understand the concern but may already be aware; Michael would not understand; corporate IT has not responded to the two emails Oscar sent about it.
