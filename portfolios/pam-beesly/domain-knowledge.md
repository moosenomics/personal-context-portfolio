# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise

**Office operations and institutional knowledge.** Pam knows how Dunder Mifflin Scranton works at a level that no org chart captures. She knows which vendor to call for supplies, which forms corporate requires for which situations, how to navigate Michael's emotional cycles, when Dwight is about to do something that requires intervention, and where everything is — physically, procedurally, and politically. This knowledge was accumulated passively over years at the front desk and is more valuable than anyone (including Pam) has formally recognized.

**People-reading and emotional intelligence.** Pam's most developed skill is her ability to read people — their moods, their motivations, their unspoken needs. She can tell when Michael is performing confidence versus actually feeling it. She can tell when Dwight is genuinely hurt versus performing outrage. She can tell when Angela is about to criticize, when Stanley is about to disengage, and when Kevin is about to make an error. This skill is intuitive and reflexive, developed through years of observation from a position where watching was more available than participating.

**Visual art (developing).** Pam has genuine artistic talent — an eye for composition, sensitivity to color and light, and an instinct for capturing the feeling of a scene rather than just its appearance. Her watercolors are her strongest medium, and her instructor has noted particular strength in architectural subjects and quiet, observational scenes. She is technically developing (the art classes are making a difference) and creatively distinctive (her voice as an artist is already present; her skill is catching up to it).

**Administrative coordination.** The mechanics of running a front desk — phone systems, scheduling, correspondence, supply management, visitor coordination — are second nature to Pam. She is genuinely good at this work in a way that makes it look easy and therefore invisible.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner

**Self-advocacy.** Pam is learning to assert herself — in meetings, in professional settings, in her own career — but the skill is new and inconsistent. She can identify what she wants more reliably than she can ask for it, and she sometimes retreats into old patterns of accommodation when the stakes feel high.

**Professional navigation.** Pam has spent her career in a single role at a single company and has limited experience with the strategic aspects of career development — networking, positioning, negotiating, self-promotion. The gap between her capability and her role is partly a result of institutional stagnation and partly a result of never having learned to close that gap deliberately.

**Technical systems.** Pam uses technology at a functional level — email, scheduling, basic document creation — but has no interest or expertise in systems architecture, data management, or the technical infrastructure she interacts with daily. Her understanding of the PCP system's vulnerability is secondhand (via Jim) rather than technical.

## Frameworks / Mental Models

Pam's primary mental model is **empathic observation.** She understands the world by watching how people behave, what they reveal through small actions, and what the gap between their words and their behavior means. This model makes her the best reader of office dynamics in the building and the worst self-advocate — because she's so attuned to what others need that her own needs get processed second.

Her secondary model is **creative perception.** Pam sees the world through an artist's lens — she notices light, composition, color, and the way ordinary things arrange themselves into something worth capturing. This model shapes how she experiences her environment and is the source of both her talent and her dissatisfaction with a job that doesn't engage it.

Her emerging model is **intentional agency.** Pam is building a new framework — one that starts with "what do I want?" rather than "what does everyone else need?" This model is in early development and competes with her deeper patterns of accommodation, but its presence is changing her decisions in measurable ways (art classes, the relationship with Jim, incremental assertiveness at work).
