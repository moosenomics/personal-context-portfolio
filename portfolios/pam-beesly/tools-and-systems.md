# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Pam's primary work tools are the phone system and her desktop computer. The phone is straightforward — a multi-line system she operates with practiced efficiency, routing calls, taking messages, and delivering the branch's standard greeting with a warmth that ranges from genuine to autopilot depending on the hour. Her computer runs the standard Dunder Mifflin corporate image, which she uses for email, scheduling, document processing, and — during quiet stretches — FreeCell.

She keeps a personal sketchbook at her desk, tucked beneath the phone log, where she draws during slow periods. The sketches are observational — office scenes, coworker profiles, the view from the parking lot — and they are better than she thinks they are. She also maintains a separate notebook for PPC planning, supply orders, and the organizational debris that constitutes the invisible half of her job.

The PCP/MCP system is a tool Pam uses practically. She filled out her profile with care, added standard exclude blocks for Michael and Dwight, and uses her AI assistant for occasional research — art supply comparisons, event planning ideas, and the kind of quick-reference lookups that a receptionist handles twenty times a day. She is not a power user and does not want to be.

## Key Workflows

Call management: Incoming calls are routed by department or person. Pam knows the routing by instinct — she doesn't need to look up extensions, she knows who's at their desk, who's in a meeting, and who's avoiding their phone. Messages are taken on paper and delivered by hand, which is less efficient than voicemail but more personal, and Pam has never been asked to change the system.

Administrative coordination: Supply orders, mail distribution, visitor management, conference room scheduling, and the hundred small tasks that keep the office running. Pam tracks these mentally and on a desk calendar that she maintains in parallel with the digital calendar she manages for Michael.

Michael management: Pam has developed an informal workflow for managing Michael's needs — anticipating his requests, preparing materials before he asks, redirecting conversations before they become problematic, and providing the emotional support he requires to function. This workflow is unwritten, uncompensated, and essential.

## Current System Issues

Pam has no system issues per se. The PCP system works as she expects it to, and she has not encountered the security vulnerabilities that others have — though she is intimately aware of them through Jim's counter-operation. She maintains the fallout log on her home computer specifically to avoid creating evidence on the office system, a decision that reflects a sharper understanding of information security than her role would suggest.
