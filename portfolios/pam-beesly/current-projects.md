## The Fallout Log — Jim's Counter-Operation
- **What:** Pam keeps a detailed log of the consequences of Jim's profile manipulation operation against Dwight. Jim planted fake expertise entries in Dwight's profile (French Impressionist art criticism, marine mammal behavioral psychology, interpretive dance therapy certification), and Pam tracks what happens when the office interacts with these entries as though they're real.
- **Status:** Active — new entries weekly
- **Pam's role:** Co-conspirator, archivist, and quality control. She monitors office conversations for Dwight-related queries about the fake expertise, documents Dwight's responses, and tracks what he's researching as a result.
- **Key collaborators:** Jim Halpert (sole co-conspirator)
- **Key detail:** Pam discovered Dwight's original profile manipulation when she overheard him muttering about "Phase 2" near the printers. She told Jim, who then discovered what Dwight had done to Michael's profile — the fake succession plan, the fabricated martial arts mentorship. Rather than reporting it, Jim began his own counter-operation using the same exploit. Pam supported this decision because (a) it was the funniest possible response, (b) Dwight's manipulation wasn't causing material harm, and (c) reporting it would end the entertainment value immediately.

The log itself is a document Pam maintains on her home computer — not on the office system, because she understands the irony of documenting a profile exploit on the same system being exploited. Recent entries include:

- Oscar asked Dwight about Monet's use of atmospheric perspective. Dwight gave a five-minute answer that was 60% accurate and 100% confident. Oscar seemed impressed.
- Phyllis asked about dolphin mating rituals. Dwight responded with clinical precision and maintained eye contact throughout, which Phyllis found unsettling.
- Kelly asked Dwight if interpretive dance therapy was "like a real thing," and Dwight said "It is as real as any other form of therapeutic intervention, Kelly" with such authority that Kelly googled it and has been considering trying it.
- Dwight has checked out a library book on dolphin social structures. Pam saw it on his desk.

Pam is prepared to shut the operation down if it crosses a line — if Dwight gets professionally embarrassed in front of a client, for instance, or if the fake credentials are referenced in a context that could cause real harm. So far, the consequences have been limited to Dwight spending his evenings studying dolphins and French painters, which Pam considers a net positive for his personal development.
