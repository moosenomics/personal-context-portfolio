# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Receptionist
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott, Regional Manager
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities

Front desk management: answering and routing incoming calls ("Dunder Mifflin, this is Pam"), greeting visitors, managing the branch's mail and package delivery, maintaining the supply closet, and serving as the first point of contact for anyone interacting with the Scranton branch. Beyond the formal job description, Pam handles a significant portfolio of informal responsibilities — managing Michael's calendar, cleaning up administrative messes, coordinating between departments, processing paperwork that doesn't clearly belong to anyone else, and serving as the office's institutional memory for anything that requires knowing where things are, how things work, or what happened last time.

Pam is also a reluctant member of the Party Planning Committee, where she assists Angela and Phyllis with event execution while maintaining the diplomatic neutrality required to survive Angela's management style.

## Reporting Structure

Pam reports to Michael, who treats her as a combination of administrative assistant, confidante, and surrogate family member. Michael relies on Pam for emotional support, schedule management, and the quiet correction of his mistakes — a dependency he would describe as friendship and she would describe as her job. Pam navigates this relationship with patience, occasional firmness, and the awareness that Michael's need for her approval is genuine even when his behavior doesn't deserve it.

Laterally, Pam interacts with every department. She is the connective tissue between sales, accounting, the warehouse, and HR — the person who knows which vendor to call, which form to use, and which coworker is having a bad day before they tell anyone. This knowledge is operational rather than political; Pam doesn't use it for advantage, she uses it to keep things running.

## Typical Week

Pam arrives on time, makes coffee for the office (a task she assumed voluntarily and has never been thanked for systematically), and settles at the front desk. Mornings are call-heavy — routing incoming calls, taking messages for salespeople who are on the phone or out of the office, and handling the initial wave of Michael's daily needs (printing, scheduling, emotional check-ins).

Mid-morning settles into a rhythm of administrative work interspersed with conversation — Jim stops by the desk frequently, other coworkers pass through, and Michael emerges from his office for the first of several unnecessary interactions. Pam uses quiet stretches for FreeCell, sketching, or small creative projects that she keeps at her desk. She eats lunch at her desk or with Jim, and the afternoon mirrors the morning with lower call volume and more time for the paperwork and coordination tasks that accumulate throughout the day.

Pam attends Michael's conference room meetings, where she sits quietly, takes occasional notes, and exchanges glances with Jim when the proceedings become absurd — which is most meetings. She participates in PPC planning when conscripted by Angela, contributing suggestions that are frequently overruled and accepting the overruling with a grace that masks genuine frustration.

## Current Extra Workstreams

Pam is taking art classes in the evening — watercolors and drawing — the first serious investment she's made in her creative ambitions. She is also Jim's co-conspirator in the profile counter-operation against Dwight, a role she performs with enthusiasm and careful record-keeping.
