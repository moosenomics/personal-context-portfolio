# Communication Style

## Overall Style

Pam communicates with quiet warmth and understated perceptiveness. She speaks less than most of her coworkers and says more with what she does say — her observations are precise, her humor is dry, and her emotional intelligence shapes every interaction even when she's not the one talking. She is the person in the meeting who sees what's actually happening while everyone else is reacting to what appears to be happening. She has a gift for reading rooms, reading people, and choosing the exact right moment to say the thing that matters — or to say nothing, which is sometimes the better choice.

## Email Style

Pam's emails are friendly, clear, and appropriately professional. She uses "Hi [name]" openings, writes in complete sentences with natural warmth, and signs off with "Thanks, Pam" or "Best, Pam." Her emails to Michael are slightly more careful — she phrases suggestions as questions and frames corrections as clarifications, managing his ego through syntax. Her emails to Jim are casual and often contain references to inside jokes that would be illegible to anyone else reading them. She does not over-email. She sends what needs sending and stops.

## Distinctive Patterns

**The knowing look.** Pam's most powerful communication tool is non-verbal — a glance at the camera (in the documentary context), a look exchanged with Jim, a facial expression that conveys an entire paragraph of commentary on whatever just happened. She communicates more through reaction than action, which makes her a natural audience surrogate but also means her actual thoughts often go unvoiced.

**Understated humor.** Pam is funny in the way that people who listen more than they talk are funny — her humor comes from precision rather than volume. She makes observations that land because they're exactly right, delivered at exactly the right moment, in exactly the right register. She doesn't compete for laughs; she just has them, and the people paying attention appreciate the difference.

**The qualified assertion.** Pam has a habit of softening her statements with qualifiers — "I think maybe," "I don't know but," "It's probably nothing, but" — that diminish the authority of things she's actually confident about. This pattern is a remnant of years of being socialized to take up less space, and she is actively, if unevenly, working to shed it. When she does speak without qualifiers — when she states something directly — people in the office pay attention because it's rare enough to signal importance.

**Empathic calibration.** Pam adjusts her communication style to match the emotional needs of whoever she's talking to with a fluency that most people don't notice. She's warmer with Michael when he's fragile, more direct with Jim when he's avoiding something, gentler with Dwight when he's been humiliated, and more patient with Angela than Angela deserves. This calibration is automatic and it's the reason everyone in the office feels comfortable talking to Pam — she makes people feel heard, even when she's the one who should be talking.

**Conflict avoidance shifting to conflict engagement.** Historically, Pam avoided confrontation entirely — she endured, deflected, and absorbed rather than pushing back. This is changing. She has begun standing up for herself in small ways — disagreeing with Angela on PPC decisions, setting boundaries with Michael, voicing opinions in meetings rather than waiting for someone else to say what she's thinking. The shift is gradual and uneven, but it's real.

## Tone by Context

**With Jim:** Relaxed, playful, and authentic. This is where Pam's real voice lives — unguarded, funny, and confident in a way she doesn't replicate anywhere else. Conversations with Jim are easy in a way that conversations with most people aren't, and the ease itself is evidence of the relationship's quality.

**With Michael:** Patient, gently redirecting, and strategically warm. Pam has learned exactly how much honesty Michael can handle and delivers it at that dosage. She is one of the few people who can tell Michael he's wrong without triggering a meltdown, though the technique requires careful calibration and sometimes fails.

**With Dwight:** Amused, occasionally exasperated, and warmer than the surface suggests. Pam treats Dwight with a mix of bemused tolerance and genuine care that Dwight doesn't fully register but benefits from.

**With Angela:** Diplomatic, compliant, and internally frustrated. Pam maintains a professional relationship with Angela through sheer self-discipline, swallowing the responses she'd like to give in favor of the responses that keep the PPC functional.

**With coworkers generally:** Friendly, approachable, and warm. Pam is the person people stop to talk to on their way to get coffee — not because she seeks it out but because she makes conversation feel safe.

## Things to Note

Pam responds best to communication that is warm, honest, and respectful of her intelligence. She does not need to be managed, flattered, or handled — she needs to be treated as a capable person whose observations matter. Her AI assistant should communicate in a friendly, conversational tone that avoids both corporate stiffness and forced enthusiasm. Match her register: direct but kind, clear but not blunt. When presenting options, frame them as real choices rather than leading questions — Pam can make her own decisions when given genuine information.
