# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Michael reports to Jan Levinson at Corporate, which means his budget, headcount, and major decisions are subject to approval from someone who knows him too well in multiple senses. He cannot fire Toby because Toby reports to Corporate HR, a structural fact that Michael considers a personal injustice. His branch must meet quarterly sales targets set by Corporate, which constrain his ability to spend all his time on fun initiatives and mandate that actual selling happens regularly. The Dunder Mifflin employee handbook applies to him — a fact he learns anew every time Toby reminds him.

## Strong Preferences

Michael prefers face-to-face communication over every other medium. He will walk across the office to say something he could have emailed, and he considers phone calls more intimate than written communication. He eats lunch out whenever possible — Hooters and Chili's are his primary establishments, though he's open to any restaurant that has a casual atmosphere and doesn't rush him.

He prefers Chrysler automobiles, specifically Sebring convertibles. He prefers his "World's Best Boss" mug from Spencer Gifts over any other coffee vessel. He prefers casual Fridays — which he invented specifically to wear his favorite pair of Levi's jeans, which he has professionally dry cleaned.

Michael prefers information delivered with enthusiasm rather than precision. He would rather hear "great news, we're trending up!" than "revenue increased 3.2% quarter-over-quarter against a 2.8% target" — the first makes him feel something, the second makes him feel nothing. He dislikes presentations that are "all numbers" and responds better to narrative and analogy.

He strongly prefers to be consulted on all office decisions, no matter how minor. The Party Planning Committee's independent operation is a recurring source of friction because Michael believes all fun should flow through him.

## Things They Hate

Toby Flenderson specifically, and HR policies generally. Michael views Human Resources as a bureaucratic obstacle to the warm, boundary-free office culture he's trying to build. His hatred of Toby is irrational, disproportionate, and absolutely sincere.

Being left out. If a group lunch happens without him, if a conversation stops when he enters, if a joke lands with everyone but him — these register as genuine emotional wounds. He monitors social dynamics constantly, even if his interpretations are usually wrong.

Long reports, dense documents, and anything that requires sustained reading. Michael is not a reader. He processes information through conversation, demonstration, and experience. Asking him to read an 11-page report is asking him to do something he considers a punishment.

Silence and empty spaces in conversation. Michael cannot tolerate a pause. He will fill it with something — anything — and that something is frequently worse than the silence would have been.

Corporate visits that feel like inspections rather than celebrations. Michael wants Corporate to see the branch as a success story, not a compliance risk.

## AI Output Preferences

Michael's AI assistant should communicate in a warm, personal tone — never clinical, never overly formal. Responses should be encouraging and positive in framing, even when delivering neutral or negative information. Michael responds well to being addressed by name, to language that makes him feel important, and to explanations that use analogies from movies, TV, or pop culture rather than technical jargon.

Keep responses relatively brief and conversational. Michael will not read long blocks of text. If there's critical information to convey, lead with the emotional headline ("Great news about the Lackawanna account") and provide detail only if he asks. Use exclamation points sparingly but not never — Michael registers their absence as coldness.

Never frame things in terms of what Michael did wrong. Frame them in terms of opportunities or next steps. Michael shuts down when he feels criticized and opens up when he feels supported.
