# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Michael's primary work tool is a desktop computer running a standard Dunder Mifflin corporate image. He uses it for email, the occasional sales report, and extensive personal web browsing. He is not proficient with spreadsheets and relies on Oscar or Angela for anything involving numbers beyond basic arithmetic. His browser history would reveal significant time spent on YouTube, Wikipedia (which he considers the gold standard of human knowledge), and various entertainment sites.

His Rolodex is arguably his most important business tool. It contains handwritten notes on every major client — personal details, preferences, family members' names, and deal history. This analog system is more effective than any CRM the company has deployed because Michael's sales approach is fundamentally relational, not transactional.

He carries a cell phone that he uses primarily for personal calls, texting employees after hours about non-urgent matters, and occasionally for client outreach. He has been known to text 911 to ensure people return his calls.

Michael now has access to the PCP/MCP system through his AI assistant, which he uses enthusiastically and indiscriminately. He reads other employees' profiles daily, looking for connection points and conversation starters. He does not appear to understand that most of the office has added him to their exclude lists, and he attributes the decreasing amount of personal information in profiles to people "still getting comfortable with the technology."

## Key Workflows

Sales leads come through a combination of Michael's personal network, cold calls (which he's surprisingly good at), and referrals from existing clients. Michael tracks deals in his head and on his Rolodex. Pam maintains the branch's official records and ensures paperwork gets filed with Corporate on time. Angela handles all financial documentation. Michael signs what Pam puts in front of him, usually without reading it thoroughly.

Internal communication happens through a combination of conference room meetings (which Michael calls frequently and with minimal agenda), desk drive-bys (which consume a significant portion of his day), and occasional emails (which tend to be long, personal in tone, and copied to too many people).

## Current System Issues

The primary system issue from Michael's perspective is that the PCP/MCP rollout has not produced the interpersonal breakthroughs he expected. People are not sharing more; they are sharing less — at least with him. Michael has submitted a formal request to Corporate asking whether the system has a "trust level" setting that he could increase, which is not a feature that exists.

Michael also struggles with the corporate expense reporting system, which he considers hostile to creativity. His monthly expense reports are a recurring source of friction with Angela and Corporate.
