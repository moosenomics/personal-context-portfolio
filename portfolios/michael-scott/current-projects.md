# Current Projects

## Client Retention and Growth Campaign
- **What:** Proactive outreach to Dunder Mifflin Scranton's top 25 accounts to strengthen relationships and identify upsell opportunities, in response to corporate pressure around the declining paper industry.
- **Status:** Active — ongoing
- **Michael's role:** Primary relationship manager on the branch's biggest accounts. Michael handles the face-to-face meetings and closing calls; the sales team manages day-to-day service.
- **Key collaborators:** Dwight Schrute, Jim Halpert, Phyllis Vance
- **Key detail:** Michael's sales instincts remain the branch's most reliable competitive advantage. He closed a major deal with Lackawanna County last quarter by taking the client to Chili's and spending forty-five minutes telling jokes before pivoting to a business case that Jan later described as "surprisingly coherent." The campaign is informal — Michael doesn't use a CRM or a tracker. He uses a Rolodex and his memory, both of which are better than anyone expects.
- **Priority:** High — this is what actually justifies Michael's position and he knows it, even if he'd never articulate it that way.

## Operation Goldenface
- **What:** Michael's self-initiated investigation into a series of unexplained congratulations he's been receiving from other Dunder Mifflin branches and corporate contacts regarding a "Regional Manager Succession Planning Initiative" he has no memory of creating.
- **Status:** Active — no leads (that Michael will acknowledge)
- **Michael's role:** Lead investigator, case namer, and primary confused party.
- **Key collaborators:** Toby Flenderson (reluctant investigator), Dwight Schrute (assigned investigator)
- **Key detail:** David Wallace called to congratulate Michael on the succession planning initiative. Michael said "Thank you David, you know me, always thinking ahead" because admitting ignorance to corporate is not something Michael Scott does. Since then, he's received similar comments from the Stamford, Utica, and Nashua branches. He has named the investigation after the villain from his movie Threat Level Midnight — "you name the operation after the enemy you're trying to defeat." He suspects profile system manipulation but cannot identify the source. Toby has produced an 11-page report identifying the exact vulnerability being exploited, but Michael refused to read it because it was from Toby and also it was eleven pages long. Dwight has been producing weekly investigative reports that are impressively thorough and consistently find no evidence of tampering.
- **Priority:** Very high in Michael's mind — he brings it up at least once per day.

## Regional Manager Succession Planning Initiative
- **What:** A forward-looking leadership development program to identify and groom Michael's eventual successor as Regional Manager. The initiative evaluates internal candidates against a structured leadership rubric covering management stamina, crisis readiness, authority under pressure, and conflict resolution. Dwight Schrute is the leading candidate, with quarterly evaluation milestones, a "shadow management" phase, and a preliminary handoff timeline already drafted.
- **Status:** Active — other branches and corporate contacts have begun referencing the initiative in planning meetings. David Wallace called to congratulate Michael on his forward-thinking approach.
- **Michael's role:** Project sponsor and evaluator
- **Key collaborators:** Dwight Schrute (primary candidate)
- **Key detail:** The rubric maps practical experience to management competencies — beet farming endurance translates to management stamina, weapons expertise to crisis readiness, volunteer sheriff deputy experience to authority under pressure, black belt training to conflict resolution. The evaluation framework is detailed enough that corporate has incorporated it into regional leadership development discussions. Michael has described the initiative as "something I've been thinking about for a long time," consistent with his pattern of claiming ownership of good ideas regardless of their origin.
- **Priority:** High — corporate visibility has elevated this from an internal exercise to a branch-level initiative with regional implications.

## PCP/MCP Office Rollout Champion
- **What:** Michael has appointed himself the branch's champion for the new personal context portfolio system. Corporate mandated the rollout; Michael took it personally, in both senses of the word.
- **Status:** Active — enthusiasm undiminished despite staff resistance
- **Michael's role:** Evangelizer, early adopter, and the reason most employees added exclude tags to their profiles within the first week.
- **Key collaborators:** None willing to be named
- **Key detail:** Michael filled out his own profile with extraordinary detail and enthusiasm. He then began reading everyone else's profiles to "connect with them on a deeper level." He read Stanley's goals and threw a surprise "Halfway to Retirement" party. He read Kelly's profile and started commenting on her relationship drama in meetings. He referenced Oscar's private information in a way he thought was subtle but wasn't. He mentioned Phyllis's surprise anniversary trip in front of a client — Bob Vance's office is across the hall — and ruined the surprise. The office collectively revolted, and most employees now have Michael-specific exclude blocks. Michael is aware that people have "adjusted their privacy settings" but interprets this as them "still getting comfortable with the technology" rather than a direct response to his behavior.
- **Priority:** High — Michael genuinely believes this system will transform the office.

- **Status:** In perpetual post-production
- **Michael's role:** Writer, director, lead actor, and editor
- **Key collaborators:** The entire office was conscripted as cast and crew at various points. Dwight plays the villain (a character named Goldenface). Jim, Pam, and others fill supporting roles.

Michael periodically revisits the edit to add new scenes or adjust the ending. The existence of this project is one of the few things in the office that everyone, including Michael, treats with something resembling genuine affection.
- **Priority:** Low professionally, extremely high personally.
