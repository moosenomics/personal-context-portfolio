# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Regional Manager
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Jan Levinson, Vice President of Northeast Sales, Dunder Mifflin Corporate
**Direct Reports:** Dwight Schrute (Assistant to the Regional Manager / Senior Sales Representative), Jim Halpert (Sales Representative), Andy Bernard (Sales Representative), Stanley Hudson (Sales Representative), Phyllis Vance (Sales Representative), Pam Beesly (Receptionist), Angela Martin (Senior Accountant), Oscar Martinez (Accountant), Kevin Malone (Accountant), Kelly Kapoor (Customer Service Representative), Creed Bratton (Quality Assurance), Toby Flenderson (Human Resources Representative — technically reports to Corporate HR, but physically present and nominally under Michael's authority, which Michael disputes on principle), Darryl Philbin (Warehouse Manager — reports to Michael loosely, operates with significant autonomy)

## Core Responsibilities

Branch P&L ownership, sales target attainment, client relationship management, personnel decisions, and serving as the primary point of contact between Scranton and Corporate. In practice, Michael delegates most operational management to the people who actually know how to do it — Angela runs accounting, Darryl runs the warehouse, and Pam runs the front desk and most of the administrative workflow that keeps the branch functional. Michael's actual contribution is client-facing sales work, morale (his word), and conference room meetings of varying necessity.

## Reporting Structure

Michael reports to Jan Levinson at Corporate, a relationship characterized by mutual exasperation and an extended period of romantic entanglement that has since ended badly. David Wallace, the CFO, occasionally checks in directly and is consistently puzzled by the gap between Michael's behavior and the branch's numbers. Dwight Schrute serves as Michael's self-appointed lieutenant, handling tasks Michael assigns him with a fervor that ranges from useful to deeply counterproductive. Everyone else reports to Michael on paper, though most day-to-day management happens laterally — the sales team self-manages, accounting operates as Angela's fiefdom, and the warehouse runs on Darryl's authority.

## Typical Week

Monday begins with a conference room meeting that is nominally about sales targets but becomes about whatever Michael watched over the weekend, a relationship issue he's processing, or a new initiative he invented in the shower. The meeting lasts 30–90 minutes depending on how strongly Stanley objects. The rest of Monday is spent making rounds — visiting each desk, attempting conversation, and being gently or not-gently redirected back to his office.

Tuesday through Thursday follow a loose pattern: Michael spends mornings in his office with the door open, alternating between actual paperwork (which he puts off) and finding reasons to leave his office. He takes long lunches, frequently at Hooters or Chili's, sometimes with clients and sometimes alone. Afternoons involve sales calls (at which he excels), impromptu meetings (at which he does not), and periodic attempts to organize group activities. He checks in with Dwight multiple times daily, often to delegate tasks that don't need delegating.

Friday is Michael's favorite day. He pushes for early dismissal, suggests group activities, and occasionally declares themed events (Casino Night, Office Olympics, Movie Monday — which he has tried to make happen on Fridays). He signs off on the week's paperwork in a burst of end-of-week energy, usually leaving several items for Pam to clean up.

## Monthly Cadence

Monthly sales reports to Corporate, which Michael dreads and procrastinates until the last possible moment. Monthly birthday celebrations managed by the Party Planning Committee (Angela), which Michael frequently hijacks. Monthly one-on-ones with each employee, which Michael treats as therapy sessions for himself rather than performance discussions.

## Quarterly Cadence

Quarterly sales targets and branch performance reviews with Jan. These meetings follow a predictable arc: Michael overpromises, the quarter unfolds chaotically, and the branch somehow meets or exceeds targets despite everything. Quarterly all-hands meetings where Michael presents numbers he only partially understands, padded with motivational content borrowed from movies.

## Annual Cadence

Annual performance reviews, which Michael approaches with the seriousness of a Pulitzer committee and the methodology of a horoscope. The Dundies — Michael's self-created annual awards ceremony held at Chili's, which is simultaneously the most dreaded and most oddly beloved tradition in the office. Annual budget negotiations with Corporate, handled mostly by Angela and Oscar with Michael's signature.

## Current Extra Workstreams

Michael is currently leading **Operation Goldenface**, a self-initiated investigation into mysterious congratulations he's been receiving from other branches and corporate contacts regarding a "succession planning initiative" he knows nothing about. He has enlisted both Toby (who is conducting a competent investigation that Michael refuses to read) and Dwight (who is the person Michael is unknowingly investigating) to help solve the case.

He is also serving as the branch's champion for the new PCP/MCP rollout, a role he gave himself and which Corporate did not request.
