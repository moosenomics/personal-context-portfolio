# Team & Relationships

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Creed's nominal supervisor, and the only person with the authority to fire him — a fact Creed is acutely aware of despite appearing oblivious to everything else
- **Interaction pattern:** Michael forgets what Creed does approximately once per quarter and has to be reminded. When Michael needs to fire someone, Creed has twice been in the danger zone and twice survived — once by redirecting the axe to Devon, and once by making himself so forgettable that Michael moved on to an easier target. Creed does not resent Michael for these near-misses. He appears to have genuinely forgotten them within days.
- **What Creed needs from Michael:** Continued employment and continued inattention. The ideal Michael-Creed interaction is one where Michael looks past Creed entirely.
- **What Michael needs from Creed:** Nothing. Michael is vaguely aware Creed exists and occasionally introduces him to visitors by saying "and this is Creed, he is in charge of... something."
- **Context:** Michael once tried to read Creed's profile to prepare for a forced bonding attempt. He stopped reading after the second paragraph and has not mentioned it since. Creed did not notice.

## Jim Halpert
- **Role:** Sales Representative
- **Relationship:** The coworker most likely to observe Creed doing something unusual and choose not to follow up
- **Interaction pattern:** Jim and Creed occupy the same office space but exist in parallel dimensions. Jim has caught Creed stealing on at least two occasions and simply noted it without escalating. Creed has offered Jim casual invitations to activities that most people would decline — hanging out at the quarry, throwing things down there — and Jim has politely deferred each time. There is an unspoken understanding between them: Jim does not ask questions, and Creed does not create problems that require Jim's attention.
- **Context:** Jim once mentioned buying a house near the quarry. Creed was visibly pleased by this development and immediately suggested they hang out there together. Jim's expression in response conveyed polite concern.

## Meredith Palmer
- **Role:** Supplier Relations Representative
- **Relationship:** The coworker Creed has the most in common with temperamentally, though he periodically forgets who she is
- **Interaction pattern:** Creed once re-introduced himself to Meredith as if she were a new employee, despite having worked in the same office for years. Meredith took this in stride. They share a casual tolerance for each other's irregularities that most of the office doesn't extend to either of them individually.
- **Context:** Creed and Meredith both occupy the office's periphery — present but not central to any group dynamic. Neither seems to mind.

## Toby Flenderson
- **Role:** Human Resources Representative
- **Relationship:** The person most professionally obligated to understand Creed and the least equipped to do so
- **Interaction pattern:** Toby has attempted to schedule meetings with Creed to discuss his anomalous profile tags (`night-operations`, `pre-2005-records`, `secondary-identity`). Every meeting has failed — Creed either doesn't show up or later claims they already had the meeting. Toby has documented these failed attempts in his own files with the weary thoroughness of someone who knows the documentation will never lead to resolution.
- **What Creed needs from Toby:** To be left alone.
- **What Toby needs from Creed:** Answers to questions that Creed either cannot or will not provide.
- **Context:** Creed is one of several simultaneous HR challenges in Toby's portfolio. Compared to the profile tampering investigation and Angela's formal complaints, Creed's anomalous tags are a lower priority — which is fortunate for Creed, since scrutiny of his profile would likely raise questions he does not want asked.

## Dwight Schrute
- **Role:** Assistant (to the) Regional Manager / Senior Sales Representative
- **Relationship:** The coworker most likely to take Creed's bizarre statements seriously and investigate further
- **Context:** Dwight is suspicious of Creed in a general sense but has never focused his investigative energy on him specifically, partly because Dwight's attention is consumed by the Jim rivalry and partly because something about Creed's affect makes Dwight instinctively wary. Creed, for his part, finds Dwight amusing and will occasionally say things to him purely to watch his reaction. Creed once accepted three dollars from Darryl to make a throat-slitting gesture at Andy, which suggests he is available for freelance intimidation work at reasonable rates.

## Devon White (Former)
- **Role:** Former coworker
- **Relationship:** The person Creed redirected a firing onto to save his own job
- **Context:** When Michael was ordered to fire someone and chose Creed, Creed talked him into firing Devon instead. Creed's argument — that he was better at his job than Devon — was not supported by any evidence, but it was delivered with enough conviction that Michael capitulated. Creed appears to feel no guilt about this and may not remember it happened. Devon remembers.
