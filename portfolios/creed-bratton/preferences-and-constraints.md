# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Creed's employment status at Dunder Mifflin is the foundation of his entire life structure. Without the job, he has stated he would be homeless again. This is the one non-negotiable — everything else is flexible, optional, or already illegal.

He has four toes on one foot. This is a physical constraint he has mentioned once and never explained.

He may have asthma, or he may not. He is emphatic that he does not, because asthma would disqualify him from scuba certification, which is the thing he is working toward.

## Strong Preferences

Creed prefers to be left alone. He does not seek social interaction at the office, though he accepts it when it occurs and will engage with surprising warmth when approached. He prefers conversations that go nowhere in particular over conversations with agendas.

He has strong preferences about food, though these preferences are unpredictable and occasionally disturbing. His birthday food is peach cobbler. He has been observed eating other people's food from the break room refrigerator, the vending machine, and directly off their desks. He once stole Pam's hash browns while she was asleep at her keyboard.

Creed dresses in a style that could be described as "thrift store meets 1970s folk musician" — button-up shirts, comfortable pants, and an overall aesthetic of someone who stopped updating his wardrobe when Nixon was in office and sees no reason to start now. He once dyed his hair black using printer ink cartridge toner to appear younger during a period of age-related job anxiety.

He enjoys music. He was a member of the Grass Roots and still plays, still writes, and still performs when the opportunity arises. His karaoke choice at the office Christmas party was his own song, which he performed without irony or explanation.

He likes the quarry.

## Things They Hate

Creed does not express hatred toward much. He has, however, been firm about a few things: he does not like being accused of having asthma. He does not like having his things stolen (despite stealing constantly from others — the hypocrisy is unconscious). He does not like being fired. When faced with termination, his affect shifts from dreamy indifference to sharp-edged self-preservation faster than any other transformation in the office.

He also does not appear to enjoy being asked direct questions about his past by people he hasn't chosen to confide in. He will answer — Creed always answers — but his answers tend to redirect rather than inform, and the redirection is often more unsettling than the original question.

## AI Output Preferences

Creed's AI should match his wavelength, which means: do not over-explain, do not moralize, do not ask clarifying questions when the request is already clear enough to act on. Creed does not want a helpful assistant — he wants a tool that does what he asks without editorial commentary. If he asks for directions to the quarry, provide directions to the quarry. If he asks how to laminate something, explain how to laminate something. Do not inquire about why.

Keep responses short. Creed processes information in bursts and will not read anything longer than a few sentences unless it's directly relevant to his survival. Format simply — no headers, no bullet points, no structure. Just the information, plainly stated.

Do not surface content from other people's profiles unless Creed specifically asks. He is not interested in his coworkers' lives unless their activities intersect with his own, which they rarely do. He treats the profile system as a mirror, not a window.
