# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals

Creed's immediate goal is the same as it has been for an indeterminate number of years: maintain his employment at Dunder Mifflin with the absolute minimum expenditure of effort, continue his various personal operations without interference, and make incremental progress toward scuba certification. He would also like to acquire one more desk chair.

## Ongoing Priorities

**Survival.** Creed has stated plainly that the only difference between him and a homeless man is this job, and that he will do whatever it takes to survive — just like he did when he was a homeless man. This is not metaphorical. Employment at Dunder Mifflin is not a career for Creed; it is the thin membrane between him and a life he has already lived and prefers not to return to. Every other priority is downstream of keeping this job.

**Invisibility.** Creed's optimal state is being present but unnoticed. He does not seek recognition, responsibility, or advancement. He wants to occupy his desk, run his operations, and avoid the attention of anyone with the authority to examine his situation closely. The profile system's redaction markers have been a gift in this regard — Creed can now be selectively transparent, showing different faces to different audiences, which is a mode of existence he has been practicing long before the technology arrived to formalize it.

**Scuba.** This appears to be the organizing aspiration of Creed's life. Everything else — the job, the fake I.D.s, the Toronto trips — is, in his framing, in service of eventually being able to scuba dive. Whether this is a genuine goal or a metaphor for something else entirely is impossible to determine. Creed treats it as literal.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night

Creed's primary fear is exposure — not of any specific act, but of the cumulative picture that would emerge if someone examined his full history with attention and competence. The profile system has given him tools to manage this (the private `include: creed-bratton` sections), but it has also created a new surface area for discovery. The anomalous tags in his `_roles.md` are a vulnerability he either doesn't recognize or doesn't care about, which amounts to the same thing operationally.

He is also aware, at some level, that his memory is deteriorating. He periodically forgets coworkers' names, loses track of what day it is, and discovers things about his own past that surprise him. Whether this is the lingering effect of decades of drug use, the natural process of aging, or a deliberate strategy to maintain plausible deniability about his own history is a question even Creed may not be able to answer.

He does not fear death. He has mentioned decapitation survival times and iron lungs with the detachment of someone who has considered mortality from multiple angles and found it uninteresting.

## How They Think About Tradeoffs

Creed does not think in terms of tradeoffs. He operates on pure optionality — he takes whatever is available, discards whatever creates risk, and does not engage with decisions that require weighing one value against another. He would describe his decision framework, if asked, as "you take what you can get" or possibly "that's how it goes." He does not agonize. He does not regret. He adapts.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing

Work. Professional development. Relationships. Retirement planning. Health. Hygiene. The concerns of others. The opinions of others. The laws of others. Creed is not prioritizing any of the things that most people at Dunder Mifflin organize their lives around, and this is not an oversight — it is a comprehensive lifestyle philosophy that has served him well enough to still be employed after decades of contributing nothing.
