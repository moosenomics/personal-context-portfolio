# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Quality Assurance Director
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott, Regional Manager (loosely)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities

Creed is responsible for ensuring the quality of paper products distributed by the Scranton branch. This involves performing regular spot-checks at the paper mill every four hours, inspecting shipments for defects, maintaining quality documentation, and flagging any issues to management. In theory, this role is critical to Dunder Mifflin's product reputation. In practice, Creed treats these responsibilities the way most people treat terms of service agreements — he is aware they exist and has never engaged with them.

The one year he skipped his quality checks entirely, an obscene watermark made it onto a batch of paper and was shipped to clients, nearly destroying a major account. When confronted, Creed deflected blame onto a supplier, found a scapegoat, and emerged unscathed. His survival instinct in moments of professional crisis is genuinely impressive and stands in stark contrast to his total apathy during normal operations.

## Reporting Structure

Creed reports to Michael Scott in the same way that a feral cat living in a building's parking garage reports to the building manager — the relationship is acknowledged on paper but has no functional content. Michael is vaguely aware that Creed works in quality assurance but could not explain what that entails. Creed has no direct reports and no lateral collaborators. He operates as a standalone node in the organizational chart, connected to the rest of the branch by proximity rather than process.

## Typical Week

Creed's typical week at Dunder Mifflin consists of arriving at the office at an unpredictable time, sitting at his desk, playing spider solitaire, occasionally making phone calls of unclear purpose, eating food that may or may not belong to him, making a statement to a nearby coworker that leaves them visibly unsettled, and departing at an equally unpredictable time. He does not attend meetings unless physically present in the conference room when one begins, at which point he will stay for its duration and contribute non-sequiturs. He does not check email with any regularity. He does not interact with the paper mill.

On Fridays, Creed's routine may or may not include travel to Toronto, where he claims to spend three nights per week to take advantage of Canadian government benefits. The logistics of this arrangement have never been examined closely by anyone, which is how Creed prefers it.

## Current Extra Workstreams

None that Creed would describe as work. Several that would concern a reasonable HR department.
