# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise

**Survival.** Creed's deepest expertise is in the art of persisting. He has been homeless, he has been in a band, he has been in cults (both as leader and follower), he has traveled the world as a musician, he has assumed at least one other identity, and he has maintained continuous employment at a paper company despite never performing his job. The common thread across all of these is an ability to read a situation, identify the minimum action required to continue existing within it, and execute that action without hesitation or guilt.

**Music.** Creed was a member of the Grass Roots during their late-1960s peak, played at the Fantasy Fair and Magic Mountain Music Festival during the Summer of Love, and contributed to multiple charting albums. He sang lead on several tracks and co-wrote others. His musical knowledge is genuine, deep, and rooted in firsthand experience with a significant era of American rock. He still plays and writes. He once reviewed a local Sweeney Todd production with the critical vocabulary of someone who has legitimate opinions about vocal performance.

**Pharmaceutical identification.** Creed can identify strains of marijuana on sight and name pain medications by appearance. This knowledge was acquired through extensive personal experience and is encyclopedic in scope. He has also demonstrated familiarity with heroin quality by geographic origin.

**Forgery and identity management.** Creed operates a fake I.D. business, has at least one alternate identity (William Charles Schneider), carries a Swiss passport as backup, and has transferred debt between identities. His understanding of documentation, lamination, and identity systems is practical and operational. He has also demonstrated facility with the profile system's technical architecture, adding custom tags and content that suggest a comfort with data systems that belies his general appearance of technological illiteracy.

**Self-preservation in corporate environments.** Despite contributing nothing to Dunder Mifflin's operations, Creed has survived every round of layoffs, budget cuts, and performance reviews. His technique involves a combination of strategic invisibility, explosive competence when cornered, and an instinct for redirecting institutional attention toward softer targets. He is, in a narrow but genuine sense, a master of organizational politics.

**Network operations.** Creed maintains contacts and connections that do not correspond to any professional or social circle visible to his coworkers. Phone calls are made to unnamed individuals regarding unnamed activities. The Toronto trips involve logistics that extend beyond government benefit collection. The quarry serves a purpose beyond recreation. The specifics of this network are not documented anywhere except in Creed's memory, which is unreliable, and in the private sections of his profile, which only his AI can read.

**Cartography, informal.** Creed has detailed knowledge of specific locations in Scranton and surrounding areas — the quarry, certain parking lots, routes that avoid major intersections — that suggests a familiarity with the local geography driven by practical needs rather than recreational interest.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner

**His own job.** Creed does not understand quality assurance in paper manufacturing at any meaningful level. He has described his role as "Quabitty Ashwoods." He does not know what a quality spot-check entails. He does not know the names of the people at the paper mill. He has successfully faked this knowledge for years, but the underlying competence does not exist.

**Technology.** Creed's engagement with computers is limited to spider solitaire, his Word-document-disguised-as-blog, and the profile system. He believes his blog is hosted at a URL that is not a valid URL. He does not understand email beyond the mechanical act of sending it. His facility with the profile system's tagging architecture is an anomaly that contradicts everything else about his technical literacy.

**Current events and social norms.** Creed's reference frame for normalcy appears to have frozen sometime in the early 1970s. He periodically makes statements that reveal a fundamental misalignment with contemporary social expectations. He does not appear to notice this gap.

## Frameworks / Mental Models

Creed does not operate on frameworks. He operates on pattern recognition built from decades of unconventional lived experience. His decision-making, such as it is, follows a single heuristic: does this action keep me alive and employed? If yes, proceed. If no, find an alternative. There is no second heuristic.
