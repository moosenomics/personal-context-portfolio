# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Creed uses a desktop computer at his Dunder Mifflin workstation, primarily for spider solitaire. He may also use it for his blog, which he believes is hosted at www.creedthoughts.gov.www\creedthoughts but which is actually a Word document Ryan set up to prevent the world from being exposed to the contents of Creed's mind. Ryan described the content as "pretty shocking, even for the internet."

Creed also has a cell phone, which he uses for calls of indeterminate purpose. He once provided a detailed theater review of a local Sweeney Todd production via phone while in the office, apparently dictating to a publication. He also has a personal vehicle — a 1980s Lincoln Town Car — from which he operates a fake I.D. business using a laminating machine acquired through non-standard procurement channels.

His AI assistant is configured to read personal context portfolios. Creed has engaged with it more actively than anticipated. He treats it like a combination of a confessional booth and a personal secretary, asking it questions that range from logistically practical ("what time does the quarry close") to existentially unsettling ("if someone were to need a new passport, hypothetically, what countries don't check fingerprints"). The AI has become a significant part of his daily routine — he talks to it more than he talks to most of his coworkers.

Creed also has access to systems and credentials that do not appear in any Dunder Mifflin inventory. His profile references software tools that the IT department has no record of deploying and access to databases that do not correspond to any known company resource. When the profile system was first set up, his `tools-and-systems.md` auto-populated with entries that confused the implementation team — they assumed it was a data migration error and moved on. It was not a data migration error.

## Key Workflows

Creed's workflow for quality assurance — to the extent it exists — involves calling the paper mill, asking carefully worded questions to extract enough information to generate a plausible report, and filing that report without ever visiting the facility. This system was developed over years of practice and represents a genuine, if misapplied, talent for information extraction.

His personal workflows — the I.D. operation, the supply resale, the Toronto logistics — are managed entirely from memory and the trunk of his car. He does not use project management tools. He does not keep written records. The absence of documentation is, from Creed's perspective, a feature rather than a bug.

## Current System Issues

Creed's profile contains tags that the system was not designed to accommodate. The tags `night-operations`, `pre-2005-records`, and `secondary-identity` do not map to any organizational role and were not assigned through any administrative process. Their presence in the system is technically an anomaly that should have triggered a validation error. It did not. Toby has noted this in his own files. The IT department has not been consulted, because no one has thought to tell them.
