# Team & Relationships

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Boss, aspirational father figure, audience for Andy's performances
- **Interaction pattern:** Andy seeks Michael's attention through agreement, flattery, and mirroring Michael's opinions. He laughs at Michael's jokes before they land, volunteers for any initiative Michael proposes, and frequently drops by Michael's office to "check in" with no agenda. Michael alternates between enjoying the validation and finding Andy exhausting.
- **What Andy needs from Michael:** Approval, belonging, the sense that he matters to someone in charge. Andy's relationship with his actual father is strained enough that Michael's intermittent warmth fills a real gap.
- **What Michael needs from Andy:** An enthusiastic audience. Andy is one of the few people in the office who will participate in Michael's ideas without visible reluctance.
- **Context:** Andy's strategy upon arriving at Scranton was explicitly to become Michael's number-two through calculated sycophancy. The overt campaign softened after anger management, but the underlying dynamic hasn't changed — Andy genuinely admires Michael's social instincts even when they produce chaos. Michael's opinion of Andy fluctuates daily between "great guy" and "please stop talking."

## Angela Martin
- **Role:** Senior Accountant, Head of Party Planning Committee, fiancée
- **Relationship:** Romantic partner, source of both Andy's greatest joy and his greatest anxiety
- **Interaction pattern:** Andy is effusively affectionate; Angela tolerates it. He brings her small gifts, sings to her at her desk, and makes wedding plans she consistently rejects. Angela's communication style with Andy is clipped and corrective. Andy interprets her withholding as "playing hard to get" rather than disinterest.
- **What Andy needs from Angela:** Reciprocated love, or at minimum, evidence that she chose him on purpose
- **What Angela needs from Andy:** Stability, social respectability, a partner who won't embarrass her at church functions — though even these modest requirements Andy frequently fails to meet
- **Context:** Andy courted Angela by serenading her with ABBA and gifting her a stray cat. She accepted his proposal with the word "Okay." Andy has not examined why her enthusiasm caps at "Okay." He is aware on some level that the relationship is lopsided but has framed this as Angela's personality rather than a reflection of her feelings. He has no knowledge of her history with Dwight beyond "they worked together."

## Dwight Schrute
- **Role:** Senior Sales Representative, Assistant to the Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** Rival, uneasy ally, occasional friend
- **Interaction pattern:** The two compete for Michael's attention and for status within the sales team. Andy initially tried to undermine Dwight to gain favor with Michael; since anger management, the rivalry has become more of a background hum. They have moments of genuine camaraderie — bonding over sales competitions, sharing a bear horn — that are interrupted by Dwight's inability to stop asserting dominance and Andy's inability to let a slight go unchallenged.
- **What Andy needs from Dwight:** Acknowledgment that Andy belongs at Scranton and isn't a second-class Stamford transplant
- **What Dwight needs from Andy:** Nothing, which is itself a source of friction
- **Context:** Dwight views Andy as a minor threat who occasionally makes himself useful. Andy views Dwight as an obstacle to the status he deserves. Neither is entirely wrong. The fact that Angela chose Schrute Farms as a wedding venue is a detail Andy finds charming rather than suspicious.

## Jim Halpert
- **Role:** Sales Representative
- **Relationship:** Colleague, former target of Andy's aggression, current source of mild pranks
- **Interaction pattern:** Andy calls Jim "Big Tuna" — a nickname Jim neither requested nor enjoys but has stopped correcting. Their dynamic is amiable on the surface but asymmetric: Jim finds Andy entertaining in small doses, and Andy considers Jim a close friend. Jim occasionally pranks Andy, but since the phone-in-the-ceiling incident, he keeps them mild.
- **What Andy needs from Jim:** Friendship, validation, someone to reference as "my buddy Tuna" in social contexts
- **What Jim needs from Andy:** Minimal disruption and occasional comic relief
- **Context:** Andy and Jim worked together at the Stamford branch before the merger. Andy's initial read of Jim as a "cut-your-throat-to-get-ahead type of guy" was wildly inaccurate but reveals how Andy sees the world — everyone is playing a status game, and the ones who seem relaxed are just better at hiding it.

## Pam Beesly
- **Role:** Receptionist
- **Relationship:** Friendly acquaintance, occasional confessor
- **Interaction pattern:** Andy treats Pam as the office's social coordinator and emotional barometer. He stops by reception to chat, asks her opinion on wedding details, and occasionally over-shares about his relationship anxieties. Pam is kind to Andy in the way people are kind to golden retrievers — she can see that he's trying very hard and it would be cruel to point out that trying hard isn't working.
- **What Andy needs from Pam:** A sympathetic ear, reassurance that his wedding plans sound nice
- **What Pam needs from Andy:** Nothing specific, but Andy's desk proximity to reception means she can't avoid the relationship
- **Context:** Andy asked Pam if there was a way to track how often his profile was viewed by other people's AI assistants. Pam said no and then told Jim, who has been considering planting a fake "most-viewed profile" award in Andy's inbox.
