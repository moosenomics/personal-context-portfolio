# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Sales Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott (Regional Manager)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities
Andy's job is straightforward: sell paper and paper-adjacent products to small and mid-size businesses in the Scranton region. This involves cold calls, client visits, maintaining existing accounts, and preparing quotes. He also participates in team sales initiatives, occasional joint sales calls with other reps, and branch-wide customer retention efforts. His actual output in these areas is inconsistent — Andy's enthusiasm for the concept of selling significantly outpaces his execution.

## Reporting Structure
Reports directly to Michael Scott, who evaluates sales performance with a generous curve and an emphasis on attitude. Lateral peers include Dwight Schrute (senior salesman, top performer), Jim Halpert, Stanley Hudson, and Phyllis Vance. Andy arrived at Scranton with ambitions to be Michael's clear second-in-command, a position Dwight considers his by divine right. The rivalry between Andy and Dwight for Michael's favor has cooled into an uneasy détente, but the underlying competition for status persists.

## Typical Week
Monday through Friday, Andy is at his desk in the bullpen by 8:45 AM, usually humming or softly singing. Mornings involve checking voicemail, returning client calls, and finding reasons to visit Michael's office for face time. Andy spends a disproportionate amount of his day on social interactions — checking in on coworkers, offering unsolicited life advice, workshopping nicknames, and attempting to organize group activities nobody asked for. Actual cold-calling tends to happen in bursts, usually in the afternoon when guilt or a glance at his sales numbers forces focus. He takes long lunches, sometimes with Michael, sometimes alone at his desk watching videos on his phone. Client visits happen one to two times per week and are Andy's strongest work product — he is genuinely warm in person, which compensates for his lack of product knowledge. Wednesdays he often tries to schedule joint calls with Jim or Phyllis, sensing correctly that he performs better with a partner. Friday afternoons are largely performative — Andy is at his desk, but he's mentally planning his weekend.

## Quarterly Cadence
Quarterly sales targets create brief periods of focused effort. Andy's numbers typically spike in the last two weeks of each quarter when urgency overrides his natural tendency toward distraction. He participates in Michael's quarterly performance reviews, which he prepares for extensively despite the reviews being largely meaningless. He also contributes to any branch-wide sales pushes Michael dreams up, usually with more enthusiasm than effectiveness.

## Current Extra Workstreams
Wedding planning with Angela Martin, which occupies significant mental bandwidth during work hours. Andy has been researching venues, booking vendors, and coordinating with Here Comes Treble for the reception — all from his desk phone and work computer. He is also actively cultivating his standing in the office social hierarchy, which he treats as a parallel full-time job.
