# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools
Andy works from a standard-issue Dell desktop running Windows, which he considers "basically the same setup I had at Cornell" despite being a completely different machine from a different decade. His desk phone gets heavy use — Andy prefers calling to emailing because it lets him deploy his interpersonal skills, which he rates far higher than anyone else does. He carries a Blackberry with a custom ringtone (his own a cappella arrangement of "Rockin' Robin") that has been the subject of at least one office incident. He also carries a personal banjo to the office periodically and stores it under his desk.

Andy uses Microsoft Outlook for email, though his inbox management is poor — he flags everything as important and then ignores the flags. He uses Excel for client tracking but his spreadsheets are more decorative than functional, featuring color-coded tabs named after sailing terms ("Starboard Clients," "Port Prospects"). He has access to the Dunder Mifflin CRM but uses it sporadically, preferring to keep client notes in a leather-bound notebook he bought specifically to look professional during meetings.

Andy's personal context portfolio is served via MCP to his AI assistant, which he has embraced with characteristic over-enthusiasm. He treats it like a personal brand document and has rewritten it multiple times. He asks his AI to help draft client emails in his voice, review his wedding venue research, and occasionally validate his life choices.

## Key Workflows
Client prospecting starts with the leather notebook, moves to a phone call, and sometimes results in a follow-up email drafted with AI assistance. Wedding planning follows no workflow — it is a constant background process that surfaces in browser tabs, desk phone calls, and whispered conversations at his desk. Internal communication happens face-to-face whenever possible; Andy will walk across the office to say something he could have Slacked.

## Current System Issues
Andy's AI assistant has surfaced information from other people's profiles that Andy finds fascinating and occasionally uses as conversation starters, which has made some coworkers uncomfortable. He does not see this as a problem — he considers it "networking." He has also noticed that people seem to know things about Dwight that Dwight doesn't mention, like an interest in French art, and has been meaning to ask Dwight about it but keeps getting distracted by wedding planning.
