# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise
**A cappella music and vocal performance:** Andy's deepest area of genuine expertise. He understands vocal harmony, arrangement, part-writing, and group dynamics from his years with Here Comes Treble at Cornell. He can identify vocal ranges, suggest harmonies, and critique performances with real knowledge. This is the one domain where Andy's confidence is actually calibrated to his ability. He can also play banjo at an intermediate level and guitar passably.

**Prep-school social dynamics:** Andy understands the unwritten rules of East Coast upper-class social environments — country clubs, sailing regattas, alumni networks, the correct way to write a thank-you note. He navigates these spaces with genuine fluency. "You give me a gift? Bam. Thank you note. You invite me somewhere? Pow. RSVP." His politeness protocol is deeply internalized and often excessive for a paper company in Scranton.

**Cornell University:** Andy possesses encyclopedic knowledge of Cornell's campus, traditions, notable alumni, a cappella history, and reputation relative to other Ivy League schools. This knowledge is both genuine and weaponized — he deploys it as social currency in contexts where it has no value.

**Office politics and alliance-building:** Andy is a more sophisticated political operator than people give him credit for. His initial strategy at Scranton (name repetition, personality mirroring, never breaking off a handshake) was explicitly tactical. The Five Families parking lot alliance demonstrated real coalition-building instincts. His execution is clumsy, but his read of power dynamics is often accurate.

**Wedding planning (recently acquired):** Through months of venue research, vendor negotiation, and Angela-driven rejection cycles, Andy has developed working knowledge of event planning, catering logistics, floral arrangements, and wedding etiquette. He could plan a solid wedding for someone whose standards aren't Angela's.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner
**Paper industry knowledge:** Andy knows surprisingly little about the product he sells. He can recite basic paper grades and Dunder Mifflin's product line, but deeper questions about GSM, coatings, printing compatibility, or industry trends will expose gaps. He compensates with charm on client calls and avoids technical discussions.

**Technology:** Andy's relationship with technology is functional but not sophisticated. He can use email and basic Office applications but has never customized a spreadsheet formula, built a presentation from scratch without help, or understood how the CRM is supposed to work. The profile system is the most complex technology he's engaged with, and he treats it like social media.

**Emotional self-regulation:** Despite anger management counseling, Andy's understanding of his own emotional landscape is shallow. He can name the triggers but hasn't developed reliable strategies for managing them. His toolkit is essentially: (1) sing through it, (2) redirect the energy into enthusiasm, (3) fail at both and escalate.

**Financial management:** Andy comes from money but doesn't understand money. He's placed multiple non-refundable deposits on wedding venues Angela rejected without budgeting for the losses. His personal finances are a mystery even to him.

## Frameworks / Mental Models
Andy sees the world through a **performance lens** — every interaction is a scene, and he's always auditioning. Meetings are shows. Clients are audiences. Coworkers are a cast he's trying to earn a role in. This framework explains both his strengths (genuine warmth in sales pitches, good instincts for reading a room's energy) and his weaknesses (inability to turn it off, confusion when the audience doesn't respond as expected).

He also operates from a **loyalty/status hierarchy** model borrowed from fraternity culture — there are pledges and brothers, and you advance by demonstrating loyalty to those above you. This explains his sycophancy toward Michael, his rivalry with Dwight, and his discomfort when he can't identify where he ranks.
