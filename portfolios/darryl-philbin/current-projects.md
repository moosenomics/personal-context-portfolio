# Current Projects

## Warehouse Operations Management
- **What:** Day-to-day management of the Dunder Mifflin Scranton warehouse — receiving, inventory, order fulfillment, shipping, equipment maintenance, crew scheduling, and safety compliance
- **Status:** Ongoing, steady state
- **Darryl's role:** Full operational ownership. Darryl is the warehouse. Every decision about how product moves through the building goes through him or through systems he built.
- **Key collaborators:** Warehouse crew (dock workers, forklift operators), Jim Halpert and Phyllis Vance (most frequent sales contacts for fulfillment coordination), Pam Beesly (order confirmations and scheduling)
- **Key detail:** The warehouse runs well because Darryl has made it run well, not because anyone upstairs provided the tools or structure to make it happen. His crew is reliable, his processes are consistent, and his safety record is strong despite Michael's best efforts to compromise it. This is the foundation everything else builds on.
- **Priority:** Always first — nothing else matters if shipments don't go out

## Shipping Route Optimization Proposal
- **What:** A restructured delivery route system that consolidates multi-stop deliveries by geographic corridor, reduces dead-head miles on the delivery trucks, and improves average transit times for the branch's top twenty accounts
- **Status:** In development — Darryl has the analysis done mentally and partially on paper but hasn't formalized it for presentation
- **Darryl's role:** Sole author. This is his idea, built from years of watching the same inefficient routes repeat themselves because nobody with the authority to change them was paying attention.
- **Key detail:** Darryl knows this proposal is good. It would save the branch real money and improve client satisfaction metrics that the sales team takes credit for. The problem is audience — Michael wouldn't understand the logistics, and Darryl doesn't have a direct line to corporate. He's waiting for the right moment or the right channel.
- **Priority:** High personal priority, but Darryl is patient about timing

## The Office Upstairs (Observation File)
- **What:** Darryl has been watching the profile system situation unfold from the warehouse with the detached amusement of someone who saw the dysfunction coming from the moment corporate announced it. He's aware that Michael started ambushing people with personal information from their profiles, that Dwight weaponized profile data for tactical advantage, and that the entire office has been engaged in an escalating redaction arms race ever since.
- **Status:** Observing
- **Darryl's role:** Spectator with commentary. Darryl filled out his own profile pragmatically — real work information, honest self-assessment, minimal drama — and used the redaction system to keep his personal goals and frustrations away from Michael and Dwight. He treats the profile system the way he treats most office initiatives: engage with the parts that are useful, ignore the parts that aren't, and watch the chaos from a safe distance.

During a routine check of the warehouse systems, he saw a reference to `night-operations` in a system log that shouldn't have contained profile tag data. He noted it, found it strange, and has not pursued it further — partly because Creed is Creed and partly because Darryl has learned that investigating weird things at Dunder Mifflin rarely leads anywhere productive. He mentioned it to no one.
