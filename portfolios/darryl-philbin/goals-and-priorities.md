# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals

Darryl's immediate professional goal is to formalize his shipping route optimization proposal and find a channel to present it — whether that's corporate, a visiting executive, or an opening created by some future restructuring. He wants to demonstrate that he can think about the business at a level above warehouse operations, because the warehouse, while important, is not where he intends to end his career.

Personally, his goal is stability: a reliable routine with Jada, a functional co-parenting arrangement with Justine, and enough financial cushion to provide opportunities for his daughter that he didn't have growing up.

## Ongoing Priorities

**Keep the warehouse running.** This is the baseline. Everything Darryl does professionally depends on the warehouse performing well, because a warehouse that runs poorly reflects directly on him in a way that an office running poorly never reflects on Michael. Darryl doesn't have the luxury of being mediocre at his core job while pursuing bigger things — the warehouse has to work first.

**Be a good father.** Jada comes before work, before ambition, before everything. Darryl's weekends with her are protected time. He will rearrange shifts, decline overtime, and push back on last-minute requests to preserve that time. This is the one area where Darryl is completely inflexible.

**Build a path upward.** Darryl knows he's capable of more than warehouse management, and he's been quietly positioning himself for the next opportunity. The shipping proposal is part of this. So is his careful management of the Michael relationship — keeping the boss happy enough to stay out of the way, while never becoming so indispensable in the warehouse that he can't be moved out of it.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night

Darryl's biggest fear is getting stuck. He's watched people at Dunder Mifflin settle into roles they never intended to keep and gradually forget they had ambitions. He doesn't want that to be his story. The warehouse is a good job, but it's not his ceiling, and every year he stays without advancing is a year he's harder to move. He's aware that the organizational structure doesn't create obvious paths from warehouse manager to anything else, and that the people with the power to change that — Michael, corporate — don't spend a lot of time thinking about warehouse talent.

He also worries about Jada — specifically about giving her a life that's genuinely better than what he had, not just incrementally better. The divorce made this harder financially and logistically, and Darryl thinks about money more than he lets on.

The profile system drama upstairs has reinforced something Darryl already suspected: the office is not a meritocracy. Advancement at Dunder Mifflin is driven by personality, politics, and proximity to power — none of which favor a warehouse manager who's physically removed from the decision-making floor. Darryl finds this frustrating in a way he doesn't often express.

## How They Think About Tradeoffs

Darryl thinks about tradeoffs practically. He weighs cost against benefit, risk against reward, and short-term disruption against long-term gain. He's comfortable making patient trades — accepting a boring year in exchange for a better position later, absorbing Michael's nonsense in exchange for operational autonomy. He does not make impulsive tradeoffs, and he does not sacrifice stability for speculative upside. The Jada factor anchors everything — any tradeoff that threatens his ability to be present for his daughter is off the table.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing

Office politics. Darryl does not invest energy in the social dynamics of the upstairs office beyond what's necessary for his job and his long-term positioning. He does not attend optional social events unless they sound genuinely enjoyable. He does not participate in gossip, does not take sides in interpersonal conflicts, and does not worry about whether Angela or Phyllis controls the Party Planning Committee. He has limited bandwidth for drama, and he allocates it judiciously.
