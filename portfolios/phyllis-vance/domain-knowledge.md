# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise
**Relationship-based sales:** Phyllis understands that in a commodity market like paper, personal relationships are the differentiator. Her sales approach — friendly, personal, patient, no-pressure — produces loyal clients who stay with her because they like working with her. She doesn't close deals through technical knowledge or aggressive tactics. She closes them because she remembered a client's daughter's recital and asked about it on the next call.

**Office social dynamics:** Phyllis has an intuitive understanding of the interpersonal currents in the Scranton branch that rivals Pam's and exceeds everyone else's. She reads rooms accurately, identifies alliances and tensions early, and understands the informal power structures that operate alongside the org chart. She knew about Dwight and Angela before most of the office. She senses when someone is struggling before they announce it. This knowledge isn't academic — it's operational.

**Event planning:** Through her PPC work (both under Angela's regime and under her own), Phyllis has accumulated practical knowledge of vendor management, catering coordination, decoration logistics, and the art of planning an event that people will actually enjoy. Her taste runs warmer and more generous than Angela's — less structured, more festive, more food.

**Scranton Business Park ecosystem:** Through Bob, Phyllis has connections and knowledge of every business in the park and many in the greater Scranton area. She knows who's expanding, who's struggling, who just hired an office manager who might need paper. This network is her competitive advantage and she maintains it actively.

**Knitting and crafts:** Phyllis knits with genuine skill. Her homemade items are well-constructed even when they're not well-received. This is a real hobby, not an affectation, and it serves double duty as a stress management technique during long meetings and slow afternoons.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner
**Technology:** Phyllis is competent with basic office tools but does not explore beyond what she needs. She would struggle with advanced spreadsheet functions, CRM customization, or any tool that requires configuration rather than just use. The profile system is the most technically complex thing she's engaged with recently, and she uses it primarily as a read-only intelligence tool rather than as something she maintains.

**Direct confrontation:** Phyllis's skill set is built around indirect influence — implication, leverage, strategic disclosure. When she needs to be directly confrontational, she's less comfortable. The Moroccan Christmas party revelation was effective but messy, and it cost her the controlled leverage she had been managing carefully. She is better at the slow game than the sudden strike.

**Self-promotion:** Phyllis does not advocate for herself effectively in formal settings. She lets Michael overlook her, lets Dwight dismiss her contributions, and lets the office underestimate her without correcting the record. Her power operates through channels that don't require public recognition, but this means she sometimes misses opportunities that would go to someone more willing to speak up.

## Frameworks / Mental Models
Phyllis operates from a **relationship capital model** — every interaction is an exchange that either builds or depletes her standing with someone. A favor stored is a favor she can call in later. Information shared selectively builds trust. Information withheld preserves options. She manages these accounts intuitively, without spreadsheets or explicit tracking, but the ledger is always running.

She also carries an **underestimation advantage** framework — she has learned that being perceived as harmless gives her access to conversations, observations, and confidences that a more visibly powerful person would be excluded from. She does not correct people who underestimate her. She uses the space their assumption creates.
