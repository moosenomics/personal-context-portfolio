# Identity

**Name:** Phyllis Vance (née Lapin)
**Role:** Sales Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton — regional branch of Dunder Mifflin Paper Company, Scranton, PA

Phyllis is a veteran saleswoman at Dunder Mifflin Scranton who operates behind a warm, maternal exterior that conceals a shrewd understanding of office power dynamics and a willingness to deploy information as leverage when the situation calls for it. She has been at the branch long enough to predate most of her coworkers and to have attended the same high school as Michael Scott — a fact Michael brings up more often than Phyllis would prefer, usually in the form of age-related jokes despite their being the same age.

She is married to Bob Vance of Vance Refrigeration, whose office is located in the same business park. Phyllis references Bob by his full name and business affiliation with a frequency that suggests either deep pride in the marriage or an ongoing branding campaign for the refrigeration company — possibly both. The marriage is robust, affectionate, and occasionally produces public displays that make coworkers uncomfortable. Bob is protective and generous, and Phyllis has built a social identity around being Mrs. Bob Vance in a way that gives her a confidence at work she might not have had before the relationship.

On the sales floor, Phyllis is quietly effective. She leverages Bob's business connections to land clients, which some coworkers consider unfair and Phyllis considers resourceful. She sits across from Stanley Hudson, with whom she shares a comfortable, low-maintenance friendship built on mutual tolerance and shared lunches. She serves on the Party Planning Committee, where her history with Angela Martin runs the full spectrum from quiet submission to outright power seizure — Phyllis currently holds the chair position through means that would generously be described as assertive and less generously as blackmail. Beneath the knitting and the gentle voice, Phyllis Vance keeps score.
