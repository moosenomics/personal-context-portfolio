# Decision Log

## General Decision-Making Approach
Phyllis decides slowly and acts at the right moment. She gathers information, considers her options, and waits for the timing that maximizes her advantage. She is not impulsive. She is not reckless. She is patient in a way that can be mistaken for passivity until the moment she moves, at which point it becomes clear she was never passive — she was waiting.

## What They Want Before Deciding
Phyllis wants to know who benefits, who loses, and how the outcome affects her standing. She doesn't need data in the quantitative sense — she needs social intelligence. Who will be upset? Who will be grateful? What does Bob think? What does this mean for the PPC? These are the inputs that drive her decisions.

## How They Handle Uncertainty
Phyllis waits. Uncertainty resolves itself more often than people think, and Phyllis has the patience to let it. She does not agonize visibly. She does not poll the office for opinions. She sits with the question, often while knitting, and lets the answer emerge. If forced to decide before she's ready, she defaults to the option that preserves the most future flexibility.

## Recent Significant Decisions

### Seizing the Party Planning Committee
- **Situation:** Phyllis caught Angela and Dwight in a compromising position after Toby's farewell party. Angela was engaged to Andy Bernard at the time. Phyllis now held information that could destroy Angela's engagement and her reputation.
- **What she considered:** Whether to tell Andy immediately, tell Michael, tell the office, or hold the information.
- **What she chose:** She held the information and used it to leverage Angela into ceding control of the Party Planning Committee. Phyllis became chair.
- **Reasoning:** Telling the office immediately would produce a single moment of drama. Holding the information gave Phyllis ongoing power — the ability to dictate PPC decisions, to direct Angela's behavior, and to occupy a position of authority she had never previously held. The calculus was clear: sustained leverage over instant gratification.
- **Outcome:** Phyllis ran the committee with relish, assigning Angela increasingly degrading tasks under the implicit threat of exposure. The arrangement held until Angela pushed back during the Moroccan Christmas party, at which point Phyllis revealed the affair to the entire office. The leverage was spent, but the chairmanship had already been established, and Phyllis retained it through institutional momentum.

### Leveraging Bob's Network for Sales
- **Situation:** Phyllis's sales numbers were solid but unspectacular. She noticed that Bob's refrigeration clients often needed office supplies and paper, and that Bob's introductions carried weight in the Scranton business community.
- **What she considered:** Whether using Bob's connections was appropriate or whether it constituted an unfair advantage.
- **What she chose:** Used them without hesitation.
- **Reasoning:** "Bob knows people. I sell paper. This is how business works." Phyllis does not share Dwight's moral anxiety about competitive fairness. She views her marriage to a well-connected businessman as an asset, the same way Dwight views his top-performer status as an asset. Everyone uses what they have.
- **Outcome:** A steady stream of warm leads and a client portfolio weighted toward local businesses with personal loyalty to Phyllis. Some coworkers resent this. Phyllis is untroubled by their resentment.

### Profile System Engagement Strategy
- **Situation:** Corporate mandated personal context portfolios. Phyllis observed how others approached the rollout — Andy's over-sharing, Stanley's under-sharing, Michael's immediate weaponization of other people's details — and decided her approach accordingly.
- **What she considered:** How much to reveal about herself versus how much access the system would give her to information about others.
- **What she chose:** A moderate profile — enough content to appear cooperative, minimal personal vulnerability. She then focused her engagement on reading other people's profiles through her AI assistant.
- **Reasoning:** Phyllis understood immediately that the profile system was an information tool, and that the people who wrote the most would be the most exposed. She wrote enough to avoid scrutiny and then used the system to learn about everyone else.
- **Outcome:** Phyllis's AI gives her useful context for conversations with coworkers and clients. She has noticed anomalies in Michael's and Dwight's profiles that she hasn't acted on but has noted. Her own profile reveals almost nothing that she wouldn't say in a hallway conversation. This was deliberate.

### What She Knows and Hasn't Shared
- **Situation:** Through a combination of AI-surfaced profile data, direct observation, and information from Bob, Phyllis has assembled a partial picture of the profile system's irregularities. She knows Michael's profile contains a succession planning initiative Michael has never mentioned. She knows Dwight's profile lists expertise areas that are obviously fabricated. She has heard coworkers asking Dwight about dolphins and French art, and she has seen Dwight's confused reactions.
- **What she considered:** Whether to tell Toby, tell Michael, tell Jim, or say nothing.
- **What she chose:** Nothing, for now.
- **Reasoning:** Phyllis doesn't know who's behind the anomalies, and she doesn't act without knowing the full picture. More importantly, the situation is producing amusing results — Dwight is visibly confused by questions about marine mammals, and Michael is playing along with congratulations he doesn't understand. There is no immediate harm, and the information may become more valuable later. Phyllis has added this to her collection.
- **Outcome:** Phyllis is sitting on partial knowledge of the profile manipulation, contributing nothing to Toby's investigation, and enjoying the chaos from a safe observational distance. This is peak Phyllis.
