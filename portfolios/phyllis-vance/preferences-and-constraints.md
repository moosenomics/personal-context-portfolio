# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints
Phyllis is a salaried sales representative with no formal authority outside the Party Planning Committee. Her budget for events requires accounting sign-off (which means working with Angela). She and Bob live in Scranton and are not relocating. Her work hours are standard — she arrives on time, leaves at 5 PM, and does not take work home. Her health is generally good, though she has a history of childhood scoliosis and occasionally hurts her back during physical activities she probably should avoid (the Café Disco incident was instructive).

## Strong Preferences
Phyllis prefers working through relationships rather than systems. She would rather call a client she knows than cold-call a stranger. She would rather have a quiet conversation that produces information than attend a meeting where everyone performs. She prefers Bob's company to anyone else's and does not pretend otherwise.

She dresses deliberately — not flashy, but put-together, and she has mentioned wearing low-cut blouses specifically to attract attention from men so that Bob will notice and respond protectively. This is strategic rather than accidental. She knits at her desk and enjoys making homemade gifts, though the gifts' reception varies from gratitude (Pam, sometimes) to polite concealment (Michael, always).

Phyllis prefers parties with real themes, real food, and real budgets — not the austere affairs Angela ran, which Phyllis described internally as "funerals with cake." She views the PPC chairmanship as an opportunity to do events properly and is personally invested in the quality of the output.

She does not like being underestimated, being called old, being compared unfavorably to younger women, or being treated as though her contributions don't matter. She also does not like when people don't know who Bob Vance is.

## Things They Hate
Michael's age jokes. Angela's condescension. Being overlooked when decisions are made. Being excluded from social plans. Being treated as background. Any implication that her sales numbers are only good because of Bob's connections — Phyllis believes, not entirely incorrectly, that converting a warm lead still requires skill.

She hates surprise changes to party plans after she's committed to a theme. She hates when people leave messes in the kitchen. She hates when her homemade gifts are obviously unappreciated. And she hates, with a quiet intensity that should not be tested, anyone who threatens her marriage or her standing.

## AI Output Preferences
Phyllis's AI should be warm but not saccharine — friendly and professional with a personal touch. Emails should include greetings and sign-offs. Client communications should reference personal details where available (birthdays, family milestones). Internal communications should be measured and noncommittal on sensitive topics. The AI should never volunteer information about Phyllis's personal life beyond what she has publicly shared. When helping with party planning, maintain a tone of confident authority — Phyllis is the chair, and her communications should reflect that. When in doubt, err toward warmth over formality, but never toward over-sharing.
