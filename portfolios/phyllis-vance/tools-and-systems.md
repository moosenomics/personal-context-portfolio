# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools
Phyllis uses a standard Dell desktop computer for email, order processing, and client management. She works in Outlook and the Dunder Mifflin CRM with quiet competence — nothing flashy, no shortcuts, no complaints about the interface. Her desk phone is her primary sales instrument, used for client calls and for calling Bob multiple times per day. She carries a personal cell phone that she uses for personal planning during work hours with the discretion of someone who has done this for years without being caught.

Phyllis knits at her desk during slow periods — scarves, mittens, and the occasional homemade gift that recipients receive with mixed enthusiasm. This is not a system in the technical sense, but it is a core tool in Phyllis's emotional regulation and time management.

Her personal context portfolio is served via MCP to her AI assistant. Phyllis engaged with the profile system more thoroughly than Stanley but less obsessively than Andy. She filled out her sections with honest, measured information — enough to be useful, little enough to maintain control over what people learn about her. She has been using her AI to draft client follow-up emails and, more interestingly, to quietly query other people's profiles for useful context. She treats the AI like a slightly more efficient version of office gossip — a tool for knowing things before other people realize she knows them.

## Key Workflows
Client management follows a relationship-driven cycle: Bob mentions a new business contact, Phyllis follows up with a call, the call produces an order or a meeting, the meeting produces an account. Internal communication happens through selective participation in group conversations, careful observation during meetings, and occasional one-on-one exchanges that produce more information than they appear to. Party planning workflows involve vendor calls, budget management with accounting (which means working with Angela, which means managing the leverage dynamic in real time), and coordinating with Michael's ever-shifting event requirements.

## Current System Issues
Phyllis has noticed that the profile system reveals more about people than some of them probably intended. She finds this useful rather than concerning. She has also noticed that Michael's profile contains a succession planning project that nobody in the office has heard Michael discuss, and that some of Dwight's listed expertise areas seem implausible. She has not raised either observation — she is content to collect information and wait for a context in which it becomes valuable.
