# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Sales Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Michael Scott (Regional Manager)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities
Phyllis sells paper and paper products to businesses in the Scranton region. Her client portfolio skews toward local businesses, many of which she acquired through Bob Vance's business network. She manages existing accounts, processes reorders, handles client inquiries, and occasionally brings in new business through referrals. She also serves as Chair of the Party Planning Committee, a role she acquired from Angela Martin under circumstances that neither party discusses casually.

## Reporting Structure
Reports directly to Michael Scott, who she has known since high school and whose management she tolerates with the practiced endurance of someone who survived four years of sharing a cafeteria with him. Lateral peers include Dwight Schrute, Jim Halpert, Andy Bernard, and Stanley Hudson. Phyllis does not compete for Michael's attention the way Dwight and Andy do — she operates through quieter channels, leveraging social awareness and, when necessary, strategically held information.

## Typical Week
Phyllis arrives on time, settles at her desk across from Stanley, and works through her client list with steady, unhurried competence. She makes calls in the morning, often mixing client follow-ups with personal calls to Bob. She takes lunch with Stanley or occasionally with Bob, who sometimes visits the office. Afternoons involve order processing, client check-ins, and the social fabric of the office — Phyllis observes more than she contributes to conversations, but what she observes, she retains.

Party Planning Committee work occupies a variable portion of her week depending on proximity to office events. When a party is imminent, Phyllis's PPC duties can consume significant time — vendor coordination, decoration planning, theme development, and the management of Angela's quiet resistance to Phyllis's authority. Between events, the committee is dormant.

Phyllis leaves at 5 PM, often with Bob waiting in the parking lot. She does not take work home. Her weekends involve Bob, their social circle, and activities she does not discuss at the office unless prompted.

## Current Extra Workstreams
Chair of the Party Planning Committee — a title she holds with more authority than Angela believes she deserves and less authority than Phyllis believes she's earned.
