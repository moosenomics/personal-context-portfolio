# Domain Knowledge

## Areas of Expertise

**Human resources policy and employment law.** Toby has a comprehensive understanding of employment law, company policy, harassment and discrimination frameworks, workplace safety requirements (OSHA), and the procedural infrastructure that keeps a branch compliant. His knowledge is both theoretical (MSW, psychology degree) and practical (years of managing one of the most HR-intensive branches in the company). He can cite relevant policies from memory, draft compliant documentation on the fly, and identify legal exposure in situations that other employees wouldn't recognize as risky.

**Conflict mediation and de-escalation.** Toby's social work training translates directly into his mediation skills. He can read emotional dynamics in a room, identify the underlying interests behind stated positions, and guide conversations toward resolution without escalating tensions. He is particularly effective in one-on-one settings where the parties are willing to engage. His limitations as a mediator are entirely contextual — he's good at this, but the branch manager actively undermines the process.

**Investigative documentation and forensic analysis.** The profile system investigation revealed a capacity for methodical, evidence-based analysis that goes beyond standard HR work. Toby traced modification patterns, identified authentication gaps, and produced a report that is essentially a security audit — thorough, properly sourced, and actionable. This suggests an analytical capability that has been underutilized throughout his career.

**Creative writing (developing).** Toby is working on a mystery novel and has enough craft to sustain a multi-year writing project with consistent quality, even at paragraph-per-day pace. His writing style is competent, observational, and character-driven — strengths that align with his professional skill set (noticing what others miss, documenting patterns, understanding motivation).

**Psychology and social work.** Toby holds degrees in both fields and applies their frameworks implicitly in his daily work — understanding personality dynamics, recognizing behavioral patterns, and assessing interpersonal risk. He does not advertise this expertise, which means most colleagues underestimate the analytical depth behind his quiet observations.

## Areas Where They're a Beginner

**Technology and systems architecture.** Toby's profile investigation required him to develop a working understanding of the PCP/MCP system's technical infrastructure, but this was ad hoc learning driven by necessity rather than existing expertise. He can describe the JSON staging vulnerability accurately but could not build or configure such a system himself. His technical knowledge is investigative — he can follow the trail, but he didn't design the map.

**Self-promotion and organizational politics.** Toby has no skill at making his work visible, advocating for his own advancement, or navigating the political dynamics that determine influence at Dunder Mifflin. He does excellent work and then fails to ensure anyone notices, which is both a personality trait and a career-limiting pattern.

**Assertive communication.** Toby knows what he wants to say and frequently doesn't say it — not because he lacks the thought but because he reads the room, anticipates the dismissal, and decides the expenditure isn't worth the return. This is a learned behavior rather than an inherent limitation, but it functions as a skill gap in practice.

## Frameworks / Mental Models

Toby's primary mental model is **procedural fairness.** He believes that consistent, documented processes are the best protection against arbitrary power — that if the rules are clear and applied equally, the people without power are protected from the people with it. This model makes him an excellent HR professional and a poor political operator, because organizations rarely run on procedure alone.

His secondary model is **quiet observation.** Toby watches, documents, and synthesizes. He builds understanding through accumulation rather than confrontation — he doesn't ask probing questions, he notes what people reveal unprompted and assembles the picture over time. This model produced the profile investigation report: Toby didn't interrogate anyone, he followed the data.

His tertiary model, applied to his personal life and creative work, is **patient persistence.** One paragraph at a time. One custody day at a time. One ignored report at a time. Toby's timeline for results is longer than anyone else's in the office, which makes him either the most patient person in the building or the most resigned.
