# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Toby's primary work tools are standard Dunder Mifflin corporate applications — email, the employee records database, and the compliance documentation templates provided by corporate HR. He maintains meticulous digital and physical files on every employee, every incident, and every complaint. His filing system is the most organized in the building, which is both a point of quiet pride and entirely invisible to everyone around him.

He uses a desktop computer in his annex cubicle, where he drafts reports, correspondence, and the occasional paragraph of his novel during quiet moments. His cubicle also holds a diploma from Temple University (MSW), a diploma from California Coastal College (BSc in Psychology), and a Penn State bumper sticker — the institutional residue of a career path that zigzagged through seminary, social work, and eventually HR.

Toby has a working understanding of the PCP/MCP system at both the user and infrastructure level. His investigation into the profile modification anomalies required him to examine the system's update pipeline — the JSON staging endpoint, the authentication model, and the audit logging (or lack thereof). His 11-page report demonstrates a more detailed understanding of the system's technical architecture than anyone in the office except possibly Dwight (who understands it from the opposite direction — as an attacker rather than an auditor).

## Key Workflows

Incident documentation: When an event occurs that has HR implications (approximately three to four times per week at this branch), Toby documents it in a standardized format, files it locally, and submits it to corporate. His documentation is thorough, neutral in tone, and written with an awareness that these records may one day be read by lawyers.

Compliance management: Annual harassment training, safety certifications, employee handbook updates, and policy acknowledgment tracking. Toby manages the calendar, schedules the sessions, and endures the inevitable disruptions when Michael decides to add his own material to a training module.

Mediation: When interpersonal conflicts escalate beyond desk-level grumbling, Toby facilitates resolution conversations. He is good at this — his social work training shows in his ability to de-escalate, reframe, and find middle ground. His success rate is limited primarily by the willingness of the parties to engage, which is in turn limited by Michael's tendency to either hijack or undermine the process.

## Current System Issues

The PCP/MCP system has a critical authentication vulnerability that Toby has fully documented and no one will address. The JSON staging endpoint accepts profile modifications without authorship verification, meaning any authenticated user can edit any profile. Toby's report recommends server-side authorship checks, comprehensive audit logging, and a retroactive review of all profile modifications. The report is 11 pages. It is on Michael's desk. It is under a magazine.

Toby has also noted that the system's standard role taxonomy does not account for several tags appearing in Creed Bratton's profile, suggesting either a system misconfiguration or an unauthorized taxonomy extension. Corporate IT has not responded to his inquiry about this.
