# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Human Resources Representative
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Corporate HR (Kendall), not the Scranton branch manager — a reporting line that Michael Scott views as a constitutional crisis
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities

Policy enforcement, employee relations, compliance documentation, conflict mediation, harassment training, onboarding, separation processing, and serving as the branch's liaison to corporate HR on all personnel matters. Toby is responsible for ensuring Dunder Mifflin Scranton operates within the bounds of employment law and company policy — a task made extraordinarily difficult by a Regional Manager who views policies as suggestions and boundaries as personal attacks.

Toby handles workers' compensation claims, OSHA compliance, diversity training coordination, and the steady stream of incident reports generated by an office that regularly features unauthorized fire drills, weapons in desk drawers, and a manager who once organized a grief counseling session about his own mortality. He processes these with quiet professionalism and files them with corporate, where they presumably join an expanding archive of Scranton-specific anomalies.

## Reporting Structure

Toby reports to Kendall at corporate HR, bypassing Michael Scott entirely. This reporting structure is the architectural foundation of Michael's hatred — Toby is the one person in the building Michael cannot fire, cannot promote, and cannot ignore (though he tries all three regularly). Toby's cubicle is positioned in the annex, physically separated from the main office, which Michael considers appropriate and Toby considers symbolic.

Toby has no direct reports but interacts regularly with every employee in the branch. His relationships are generally amicable — people come to Toby with complaints, questions, and interpersonal issues that they can't or won't bring to Michael. He functions as the branch's unofficial counselor, a role he's qualified for (MSW from Temple) but not compensated for.

## Typical Week

Toby arrives at a normal hour, makes coffee, and settles into his annex cubicle. Mondays typically involve processing whatever HR implications emerged from the previous week — incident reports, complaint follow-ups, policy clarifications triggered by Michael's Friday activities. He attends Michael's conference room meetings when required, contributing carefully worded suggestions that are immediately dismissed or redirected.

Mid-week is dominated by ongoing casework: mediation between employees, documentation of workplace incidents, and communication with corporate. Toby takes notes during meetings, maintains employee files, and quietly handles the administrative machinery that keeps the branch from being shut down by legal or compliance.

Toby eats lunch alone or occasionally with other employees who don't mind his company. He uses fragments of free time to work on his novel — a few sentences between tasks, a paragraph during a slow afternoon. He leaves at a normal hour, picks up Sasha when it's his custody day, and goes home to a life he experiences with the same mild resignation he brings to work.
