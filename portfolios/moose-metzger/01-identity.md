# Identity

**Name:** Mike "Moose" Metzger, Jr.
**Role:** Director of Strategic Innovation & Owner
**Organization:** Regional HVAC distributor — family-owned, exclusive territory for a major manufacturer, four branch locations in Minnesota

Moose has been working at the family business since he was 14 years old, breaking forklifts in the warehouse before anyone trusted him with a keyboard. After college he came on full-time and spent the next fifteen years as Director of IT, during which he built — from scratch, with no programming experience beyond Excel VBA macros — an ecommerce platform and product knowledge graph that became the company's competitive moat for two decades. Then he left to build Fantasy National Golf Club, a DFS golf analytics platform with a Stripe subscription model. He sold it to SportsHub Games Network, watched it get acquired by Betsperts Media & Technology Group, and six years later came back to the family business with a new title and a new mission: figure out how AI changes everything.

He has zero direct reports and no HVAC technical knowledge. His job is to see around corners, build capabilities the organization doesn't know it needs yet, and prove concepts with working demos rather than slide decks. He operates through influence, not org chart authority — which suits him fine because he'd rather build something and show it than sit in a meeting and explain it.

Moose thinks in systems, schemas, and abstractions. He is not precious about being called a programmer or an AI expert — he's a strategic operator who builds things. The things he builds tend to be more rigorous than anyone asked for, and they tend to compound in value far beyond the original problem. That's the pattern, and it's been running for twenty years.
