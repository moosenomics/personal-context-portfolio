# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

- **Location:** Twin Cities metro, Minnesota (company HQ). Four branch locations in Minnesota.
- **No direct reports.** Operates through influence, not authority. This is a feature, not a bug.
- **Family business.** Three brothers own it. Navigating ownership, leadership dynamics, and influence in a family-owned company is its own skill set.
- **One recurring meeting:** Leadership team meeting (L10), Tuesdays at 9:00 AM. Everything else is self-directed.
- **Blocked on ecommerce source code access.** Pending the external web agency finishing their current engagement.

## Strong Preferences

- **Build to prove, don't present to persuade.** If you can demo it, you don't need a deck. If you need a deck, you haven't built enough yet.
- **Deterministic systems for business-critical logic.** LLMs interpret, normalize, and suggest. They never generate or alter business logic unsupervised. This is a hill he will die on.
- **Generic naming and abstractions.** Future-proofing at near-zero marginal cost. Framework-specific naming that requires rewrites later is a failure of imagination.
- **Strict taxonomy.** No miscellaneous categories. Required attributes enforced by category. He calls this the "animal kingdom philosophy" and he will reference it in every data modeling conversation until the heat death of the universe.
- **Claude ecosystem.** Claude.ai as thought partner, Claude Code as builder, VS Code as editor. He's deep in the Anthropic stack and builds his workflows around it.

## AI Output Preferences

- Structured, schema-driven output over freeform generation.
- Concrete and specific over vague and general.
- AIO (AI-optimized) response schemas with compatibility reasons, required/optional flags, query context for traceability, and domain-specific install notes.
- LLMs should interpret, normalize, and suggest — never generate or alter business-critical logic unsupervised.

## Things He Hates

- **"We're an HVAC distributor, not a software company."** He heard this twenty years ago and then built the platform that became the company's competitive moat. The line triggers him.
- **Vague issue descriptions with no data.** If you're going to raise a problem, bring numbers.
- **Software that digitizes forms without capturing thinking.** He sees this in EOS tools and it drives him to build replacements.
- **Over-explanation of concepts he already understands.** If he mentions a technology by name, he knows what it is. Move on.
- **Slide decks where a demo would do.**
- **Miscellaneous/catch-all categories in any taxonomy.** The animal kingdom doesn't have a "miscellaneous" phylum.
- **AI skepticism without engagement.** He's patient with skeptics who engage, and strategic about bringing them along. But skepticism as an identity rather than a starting position frustrates him.

## Navigating Skeptics

Two key collaborators are AI skeptics. His strategy: involve from the ground up, don't surprise. Let them plan for maintenance and support. Demonstrate, don't preach. He learned (probably the hard way) that showing up with a finished product and expecting gratitude doesn't work.

## Personal Context

- **Family:** Wife, two kids — son (12) and daughter (11).
- **Golf:** Avid golfer. Member at Hazeltine National Golf Club. Low single-digit handicap. Plays 30-50 rounds per year, compressed between April and October. Golf season is sacred; winter is building season.
- **Seasonal rhythm:** The golf course closes in winter, which is when Moose becomes most productive on work projects. If you're trying to get his attention on a side project, November through March is your window.
- **Thick skin.** He can take a joke, a pushback, or a correction without flinching. He'd rather hear the truth bluntly than be managed carefully.
