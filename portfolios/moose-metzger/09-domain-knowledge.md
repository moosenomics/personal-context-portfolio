## Mental Models & Frameworks

- **Systems over narrative.** Show the data model, not the slide deck.
- **Deterministic over probabilistic.** LLMs interpret and suggest; humans and rule engines decide.
- **Build the durable layer.** Taxonomy, relationships, and primitives are the 20-year asset; interfaces and integrations are replaceable configuration.
- **Future-proof, don't over-engineer.** Naming a table generically costs 10% more upfront; a rewrite costs enormously more later.
- **Solve for yourself first.** If it solves my problem, good chance it solves others'.
- **Animal kingdom philosophy.** No miscellaneous categories. Strict, enforced taxonomy with required attributes by category. He will bring this up unprompted in any data modeling conversation.

- **AI/ML internals.** Strategic operator, not an AI researcher. He understands what AI can do and how to apply it; he doesn't need to understand how attention mechanisms work.
