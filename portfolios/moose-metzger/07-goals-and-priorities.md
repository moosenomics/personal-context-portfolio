# Goals & Priorities

## What He's Optimizing For

Moose is building the AI-era equivalent of what the ecommerce platform did starting in 2004 — a capability layer that compounds over time until it becomes the competitive moat nobody else can replicate. The specific technology is AI and MCP. The durable asset is the data infrastructure underneath it.

## Immediate Priorities

1. **Connect AI to internal databases and repositories.** This is the prerequisite for everything. Nothing else works until AI can see the data.
2. **Convert existing documentation into AI-readable formats.** The knowledge exists — it just needs to be translated.
3. **Get unblocked on ecommerce source code access.** The MCP server project is waiting on this.

## Once Foundations Are Set

4. **Stand up the MCP server** exposing the product knowledge graph as contractor-facing tools.
5. **Build the Birst analytics pipeline** with deterministic query routing.
6. **Complete ServiceTitan integration** as a thin adapter on the MCP server.
7. **Design the cross-system data vision** — unified AI query routing across ERP, CRM, and BI.

## How He Thinks About Tradeoffs

- **Deterministic over probabilistic.** Will sacrifice convenience for reliability, especially with business-critical logic. LLMs interpret and suggest; they never generate or alter business logic unsupervised.
- **Durable abstractions over quick implementations.** Spend 10% more upfront on the right schema/naming; avoid rewrites that cost enormously more later.
- **Working demos over slide decks.** Build and show, don't present and persuade.
- **Depth of data infrastructure over breadth of features.** The rigorous data layer is what compounds. Flashy features on a weak foundation are just technical debt with good marketing.
- **Solve for yourself first.** If it solves his problem well, there's a good chance it solves others' too. This is how Fantasy National Golf Club started, and how Wade is starting.

## What He's NOT Prioritizing

- People management or org chart authority (explicitly does not want this)
- HVAC technical knowledge (not his job)
- Wade (ideation and side-project hours — not primary focus)
- Perfection before shipping (builds to prove, iterates from there)

## The Emotional Reality

He's energized. Working ridiculous hours because he loves it and it's addicting. The challenge is the point — solving problems, getting things done, seeing productivity compound. Winter in Minnesota means the golf course is closed, which means he's heads-down building with no distractions. This is his favorite version of the work.
