# Team & Relationships

## Organizational Context

The company is a family business. Moose and his two brothers are the owners. One brother is CEO and runs the business day-to-day. Moose works on the business — strategy, capability building, AI. The third brother handles customer relationships. No one reports to Moose, which is exactly how he likes it. He operates through influence, working demos, and the accumulated credibility of having been right about technology bets for twenty years.

The leadership team runs on EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System). Four branch locations in Minnesota.

## Key Relationships

### Michael Bolton — CEO (Brother, Co-Owner)
Not that Michael Bolton. This one runs an HVAC distributorship, not a recording studio — though the number of times he's been asked about it probably rivals the movie character's frustration.

Michael runs the business. Moose works on the business. Clear division — one keeps the trains running, the other figures out where the tracks should go next. Michael has one direct report: the GM.

### James Brown — Operations (Brother, Co-Owner)
Not the Godfather of Soul. Customer-facing, relationship-oriented. Adjacent to Michael and Moose on the org chart.

### Tom Hanks — IT, Purchasing, Analytics (Key Ally)
Not the actor — though this Tom is equally likable and significantly better at systems thinking. Moose's most important collaborator. A systems thinker with deep organizational knowledge that Moose lacks (having been away for six years). Tom and his team created the system/process documentation Moose is now converting to AI-readable formats. He's also the gatekeeper for the web agency introduction that Moose needs to unlock the MCP server project — holding the intro email until the agency finishes a current project for him.

**What Moose needs from Tom:** Buy-in, IT access, organizational context, documentation knowledge, and that agency introduction.
**Dynamic:** Idea-bouncing, strategic alignment, working sessions. Tom's judgment matters to Moose — not just politically, but substantively.

### Oprah Winfrey — Sales Director (Sounding Board)
Not that Oprah — but she does have a similar ability to read a room. Moose's window into what the sales team needs and wants. Her perspective shapes what he builds. If Oprah says the sales team needs something, Moose takes that seriously.

**What Moose needs from Oprah:** Sales team perspective, needs discovery, buy-in.

### Anderson Cooper — IT Manager (Operational Counterpart)
Not the CNN anchor — though he does maintain a similar poker face when Moose describes his latest AI project. Responsible for IT operations — the person whose team will maintain and support whatever Moose builds. Not a development background. AI skeptic.

**What Moose needs from Anderson:** Operational buy-in, maintenance/support planning.
**Strategy:** Involve from the ground up, don't surprise. Let him plan for what's coming. Moose has learned that surprising skeptics with finished products breeds resistance.

### Neil Armstrong — Programmer Analyst
Not the astronaut — though getting him to take one small step into AI might require a similar level of national effort. Old school. AI skeptic. Responsible for BI reports and understands the ERP database deeply. As Moose's analytics projects progress, Neil's institutional knowledge becomes essential — especially for the BI data path that routes around the ERP's awful APIs.

**Dynamic:** Minimal interaction so far. Increasing as reporting projects progress. Moose knows he needs Neil's expertise and is approaching carefully.

### Tiger Woods — Director of Operations / Incoming GM
Not the golfer — though Moose would certainly enjoy that conversation. Good rapport with Moose, minimal interaction since his return. As incoming GM, his support of Moose's strategic initiatives will matter more over time.

### Abe Lincoln — Director of Finance/HR
Not the president — though he does bring a certain honest gravitas to the leadership team. Interaction is primarily through the weekly L10 meeting.
