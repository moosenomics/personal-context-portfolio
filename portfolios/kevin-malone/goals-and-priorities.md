# Goals & Priorities

## Current Goals

Getting over Stacy. The breakup is recent enough that Kevin still feels it — the empty apartment, the unshared meals, the absence of someone who said yes when three other women said no. He doesn't talk about it much at work, but the sadness surfaces in quiet moments and in the slightly higher frequency with which he seeks out food, music, and company.

Growing Scrantonicity II. Kevin wants more gigs, tighter sets, and the feeling that his musical life is moving forward even when his professional life is standing still. The band is where Kevin experiences mastery, and he's chasing that feeling with more energy than he brings to anything at Dunder Mifflin.

Being happy. This sounds generic but for Kevin it is specific and intentional — he has organized his life around the pursuit of enjoyment (food, music, friendship, poker) and he is not interested in reorganizing it around achievement, advancement, or anyone else's definition of success.

## Ongoing Priorities

Keeping his job. Kevin is not oblivious to his performance issues — he just contextualizes them differently than Angela does. He knows he makes mistakes. He knows Angela corrects them. He considers this a functioning system rather than a failing one, and he is motivated to maintain it by showing up, doing the work, and staying below the threshold where termination becomes a serious conversation.

Food. This is not a joke priority — Kevin's relationship with food is one of the most authentic things about him. He plans meals, researches recipes, cooks with genuine skill (the chili is evidence), and experiences eating with a presence that most people reserve for more "serious" activities. Food is Kevin's meditation, his creative outlet, and his primary source of daily happiness.

Friendship. Kevin is not a loner — he values the office relationships, the band, the poker games, and the casual warmth of being around people who know him. He doesn't need deep conversation or emotional processing; he needs proximity, shared experience, and someone to eat lunch with.

## Fears / What Keeps Them Up at Night

Being alone. The Stacy breakup sharpened this fear. Kevin wants a partner, wants to be chosen, and has now been unchosen in a way that confirms his suspicion that the choosing was a miracle and the unchoosing was the correction.

Being fired. Kevin knows his job performance is not where Angela wants it. He doesn't think about this constantly, but it's there — a background awareness that his position is more fragile than he pretends and that the safety net of Michael's fondness has limits he can't fully gauge.

Not being taken seriously. Kevin presents as someone who doesn't care what people think, and in most contexts that's accurate. But being treated as the office joke — the dumb one, the slow one, the one whose contributions don't count — lands in a place Kevin doesn't show. He responds by doubling down on the persona (leaning into the humor, the food, the simplicity) rather than fighting the perception, because fighting the perception would require vulnerability he's not equipped to manage.

## How They Think About Tradeoffs

Kevin doesn't think about tradeoffs in any structured way. He follows the path that produces the most immediate positive feeling — the meal that sounds best, the activity that seems most fun, the response that avoids the most conflict. This produces excellent results in domains where enjoyment and quality are aligned (cooking, music) and poor results in domains where discipline and accuracy are required (accounting, personal finance). Kevin does not experience this asymmetry as a pattern; he experiences it as some things going well and other things going less well.

## What They're NOT Prioritizing

Career development, financial planning, health and fitness (he is aware of this and unbothered by it), professional skill-building, or any form of self-improvement that requires sustained discomfort. Kevin has made peace with who he is, and while this peace is sometimes mistaken for complacency, it is — from Kevin's perspective — the most rational decision he's ever made.
