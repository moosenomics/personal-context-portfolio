# Tools & Systems

## Primary Tools

Kevin's primary work tool is his desktop computer, which he uses for accounting data entry, email, FreeCell, and checking sports scores — roughly in that order of frequency, though the order Angela assumes is the reverse. He has a desk calculator that he uses for quick math, though his quick math is not always quick or correct. His M&M jar doubles as a desk accessory and a caloric commitment.

Kevin uses the standard Dunder Mifflin accounting software for transaction processing and ledger maintenance. His relationship with the software is functional but shallow — he knows which screens to use and which buttons to click, but when something behaves unexpectedly, he either asks Oscar or tries things until it works again.

The PCP/MCP system confused Kevin. He filled out his profile incompletely and with several factual errors (he listed his job title as "Accountant and Drummer," which is only half correct by Dunder Mifflin standards). He added Michael and Dwight to his exclude lists because everyone else did. He asked his AI assistant if it could recommend pizza places, which it could, and this has been the primary value the system provides him.

## Key Workflows

Accounting workflow: Angela assigns entries, Kevin processes them, Angela reviews and corrects. The workflow is circular by design — Angela builds in the review step because Kevin's output requires it. Kevin's processing speed is adequate; his accuracy is the variable. He works steadily through assigned tasks and flags items he doesn't understand for Oscar rather than guessing (a practice he adopted after Angela made it clear that guessing was worse than asking).

Food workflow: Kevin plans meals with more care than he plans spreadsheets. He researches recipes, prepares elaborate lunches, and approaches the office vending machine with the strategic consideration of someone allocating resources across competing priorities.

## Current System Issues

Kevin doesn't have system issues because Kevin doesn't use systems at a level where issues emerge. His PCP profile is incomplete, his understanding of the redaction feature is minimal, and his primary interaction with the AI assistant involves restaurant recommendations. The system works fine for Kevin because Kevin's expectations are very low.
