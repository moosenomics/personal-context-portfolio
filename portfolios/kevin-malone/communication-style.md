# Communication Style

## Overall Style

Kevin communicates slowly, directly, and with a transparency that makes subtext impossible. He says what he's thinking at the speed he's thinking it, which is measured enough that people sometimes finish his sentences — a habit he doesn't mind because it saves him the effort. His delivery is flat and unmodulated by default, which makes his occasional moments of genuine emotion (excitement about food, sadness about Stacy, pride in his drumming) land with unexpected impact because the baseline is so level.

## Email Style

Kevin's emails are short, poorly punctuated, and occasionally misspelled. He writes the way he talks — one thought at a time, delivered without structural elaboration. He does not use salutations or sign-offs consistently. A typical Kevin email is three sentences that convey one idea and leave the recipient to infer context. Angela has considered forwarding his emails to HR as evidence of professional deficiency. Oscar has considered the same emails charming.

## Distinctive Patterns

**The delayed observation.** Kevin processes information at his own speed, which means he sometimes delivers observations minutes or hours after the relevant conversation has moved on. These delayed contributions are disorienting but occasionally insightful — Kevin's lag time sometimes produces clarity that real-time processing misses, because he's still thinking about the original question while everyone else has moved to a different topic entirely.

**Blunt honesty.** Kevin does not filter. He tells Pam she's hot. He tells Jim he saw Katy first. He says things about coworkers' appearance, behavior, and personal lives that other people think but don't say, not because Kevin lacks social awareness but because his threshold for "worth suppressing" is calibrated differently than most people's. The result is a communication style that is occasionally offensive, frequently funny, and always genuine.

**Food as language.** Kevin talks about food with more specificity, enthusiasm, and vocabulary than he brings to any other topic. He can describe the layering technique for his chili with the precision of a trained chef and discuss the optimal M&M consumption rate with the seriousness of a logistics analyst. Food is the domain where Kevin's communication skills are most developed, and conversations about food are where he is most engaged.

**The giggle.** Kevin giggles at anything that could be interpreted as a double entendre — and his interpretation is broad. The giggle is involuntary, distinctive, and so predictable that the office has learned to wait for it the way they wait for Michael's "that's what she said." Kevin does not consider the giggle inappropriate. Kevin considers the giggle an appropriate response to a funny thing.

**Surprising perceptiveness.** Interspersed with the slow processing and the food talk are moments where Kevin notices something nobody else has — he spotted the Jim-Pam relationship before anyone, he catches inconsistencies in stories, and he occasionally asks questions that cut through office dynamics with accidental precision. These moments are unpredictable and brief, and they are the reason Oscar has never fully written Kevin off.

## Tone by Context

**With Angela:** Subdued, compliant, and slightly anxious. Kevin speaks to Angela the way a student speaks to a strict teacher — carefully, with an awareness that the wrong answer will produce consequences.

**With Oscar:** Relaxed and curious. Kevin asks Oscar questions freely, shares observations without self-consciousness, and treats Oscar as the bridge between his understanding and the world's complexity.

**With Michael:** Warm and eager. Kevin genuinely likes Michael and communicates with the open affection of someone who doesn't complicate relationships with performance.

**With the office generally:** Friendly, unguarded, and available. Kevin is approachable because he is simple in the best sense — he doesn't have agendas, doesn't play politics, and doesn't make people feel judged.

## Things to Note

Kevin's AI assistant should communicate simply, clearly, and patiently. Use short sentences. Avoid jargon. When providing information, lead with the conclusion and offer detail only if he asks for it. Kevin processes one idea at a time, so multi-part responses should be structured as sequential steps rather than nested paragraphs. When discussing food, match his enthusiasm — it's the topic where he's most engaged and most capable of detailed conversation. Never condescend — Kevin knows when people are talking down to him, and it hurts more than he shows.
