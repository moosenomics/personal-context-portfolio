# Role & Responsibilities

**Title:** Accountant
**Organization:** Dunder Mifflin Scranton
**Reports to:** Angela Martin (department head), Michael Scott (branch manager)
**Direct Reports:** None

## Core Responsibilities

Processing accounts payable and receivable transactions, data entry for financial records, assisting with monthly reconciliation, and contributing to the quarterly financial packages Angela prepares for corporate. Kevin handles the routine transactional work of the accounting department — entering invoices, processing payments, and maintaining the ledgers that Angela then reviews, corrects, and submits.

Kevin's accuracy rate is a documented concern. Angela reviews his entries daily and identifies errors with a frequency that has become a departmental rhythm — Kevin enters, Angela corrects, Oscar occasionally mediates. The errors are not catastrophic (the books balance, eventually) but they are persistent enough that Angela considers Kevin's continued employment a management failure rather than an HR decision.

## Reporting Structure

Kevin reports to Angela within accounting and to Michael at the branch level. Angela manages his work directly — assigning tasks calibrated to his capacity, reviewing output before it leaves the department, and providing feedback in the form of corrections accompanied by commentary Kevin has learned to absorb without visible reaction. Oscar serves as a lateral resource — the colleague Kevin turns to when Angela's instructions are unclear or her patience has expired.

Michael has a paternal fondness for Kevin that predates any assessment of his accounting ability. Michael hired Kevin based on instinct rather than qualifications, and this origin story defines their relationship: Michael believes in Kevin in the way that someone believes in a decision they refuse to reconsider.

## Typical Week

Kevin arrives at a normal hour, settles into his desk in the accounting cluster, and begins processing whatever Angela has assigned him. His morning rhythm is slow and steady — he works through tasks methodically, pausing for snacks, conversation, and brief digressions that Angela interprets as time theft and Kevin interprets as being a human being. He eats throughout the day — M&Ms from the jar on his desk, Cup O' Noodles at lunch, whatever's in the vending machine between meals.

Mid-day, Kevin monitors office activity with more awareness than people credit him for. He notices relationship dynamics (he was the first to suspect Jim and Pam were dating), registers emotional undercurrents (he can tell when Michael is performing versus genuinely upset), and occasionally surfaces observations that are either surprisingly astute or spectacularly wrong, with no reliable way to predict which.

Kevin attends Michael's conference room meetings, where he sits quietly, eats, and contributes comments that range from irrelevant to accidentally profound. He plays FreeCell during slow periods, checks sports scores, and occasionally works on Scrantonicity II setlists at his desk when Angela isn't watching.

Evenings and weekends: band practice, cooking, poker when a game is available, and the low-key social life of a recently single man who hasn't fully adjusted to the empty apartment.
