# Team & Relationships

## Angela Martin
- **Role:** Senior Accountant / Department Head
- **Relationship:** Kevin's direct supervisor and the person who most consistently communicates that he is not meeting expectations.
- **Interaction pattern:** Angela assigns Kevin work, reviews it, corrects it, and delivers the corrections with varying degrees of contempt. Kevin absorbs this treatment with a resilience that looks like obliviousness but is probably something closer to acceptance — he knows Angela thinks he's bad at his job, he disagrees (mildly), and he has decided that arguing about it would take more energy than the disagreement is worth. He once pointed out that Angela is mean, and she replied that she is "demanding" — a distinction Kevin accepted without further comment.
- **What Kevin needs from Angela:** Clearer instructions, more patience, and the occasional acknowledgment that he got something right.
- **What Angela needs from Kevin:** Fewer errors. Ideally, a different employee.
- **Context:** Angela has excluded Kevin from PPC budget discussions and has filed complaints about his accuracy. Kevin is aware of the exclusion and interprets it as Angela being Angela rather than a statement about his competence.

## Oscar Martinez
- **Role:** Accountant
- **Relationship:** Kevin's closest work friend and the colleague he turns to when he needs something explained — which is frequently.
- **Interaction pattern:** Oscar and Kevin have a functional, mildly affectionate dynamic. Oscar explains things to Kevin with the patience of someone who has calibrated to Kevin's processing speed and accepted it. Kevin asks questions that are sometimes naive and sometimes surprisingly sharp, and Oscar has learned not to assume which one is coming. They sit near each other, eat lunch together occasionally, and share the quiet camaraderie of people who spend eight hours a day in a three-person department with Angela Martin.
- **What Kevin needs from Oscar:** Translation — Kevin needs Oscar to bridge the gap between what Angela says and what Kevin understands.
- **What Oscar needs from Kevin:** Nothing professionally, but Kevin provides companionship and a low-key friendship that Oscar values more than his occasional sighs suggest.

## Michael Scott
- **Role:** Regional Manager
- **Relationship:** The man who hired Kevin based on a feeling, and the boss who has never reconsidered that decision despite all available evidence.
- **Interaction pattern:** Michael treats Kevin with a warmth that is partially paternal and partially the loyalty of someone defending an investment they can't admit was wrong. He includes Kevin in activities, laughs at Kevin's jokes (when he gets them), and periodically subjects Kevin to well-meaning but uncomfortable physical interactions (Kevin sitting on Michael's lap during the Santa episode remains a shared trauma for both).
- **What Kevin needs from Michael:** Continued employment and the kind of acceptance that doesn't require Kevin to be something he isn't.
- **What Michael needs from Kevin:** A loyal employee who makes Michael feel like a good judge of character.

## Jim Halpert
- **Role:** Sales Representative
- **Relationship:** A friendly colleague Kevin admires and occasionally annoys.
- **Interaction pattern:** Kevin enjoys Jim and Pam as a couple — partly because he was right about them dating before anyone else confirmed it, and partly because their initials are PB&J, which Kevin finds delightful on multiple levels. He interacts with Jim casually, enjoys the pranks on Dwight, and occupies the role of enthusiastic bystander in most office dynamics.
- **Context:** Kevin was the first person to suspect Jim and Pam were together and was determined to prove it. He considers this his greatest investigative achievement.

## Scrantonicity II Bandmates
- **Role:** Musical collaborators
- **Relationship:** Kevin's creative community outside the office.
- **Interaction pattern:** Band practice is a weekly commitment Kevin takes seriously — he shows up on time (unlike work), prepared (unlike accounting), and emotionally invested (unlike most things in his professional life). The band is where Kevin experiences competence without qualification, and the contrast between how he feels behind a drum kit and how he feels behind an accounting spreadsheet is significant.
