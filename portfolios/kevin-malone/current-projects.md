# Current Projects

## Quarterly Financial Processing Support
- **What:** Kevin's portion of the quarterly financial close — data entry, transaction processing, and ledger maintenance for the accounts Angela assigns to him.
- **Status:** Active — ongoing
- **Kevin's role:** Junior processor. Angela assigns him the transactional work, reviews his output, corrects errors, and incorporates the cleaned results into the quarterly package.
- **Key detail:** Kevin's contribution to the financial close is genuine but uneven. On good weeks, his entries are mostly correct and Angela's review is a formality. On bad weeks, the error rate spikes and Angela's corrections consume time she'd rather spend on higher-level analysis. Kevin is aware of this pattern but experiences it as fluctuation rather than a problem to solve — some weeks the numbers cooperate and some weeks they don't.
- **Priority:** High by obligation, moderate by engagement.

## Scrantonicity II
- **What:** Kevin's Police cover band, formed after a creative split from the original Scrantonicity.
- **Status:** Active — rehearsing, booking gigs
- **Kevin's role:** Drummer, lead singer, creative director, and the reason the band exists.
- **Key detail:** The split from Scrantonicity was painful but necessary — the original band wanted to expand beyond The Police into broader classic rock, and Kevin was unwilling to compromise on the band's identity. Scrantonicity II plays exclusively Police songs and Kevin considers this artistic purity, not stubbornness. The band performed at Phyllis and Bob Vance's wedding (the third wedding they've played) and is actively seeking more bookings. Kevin's drumming is genuinely skilled — one of several areas where his competence significantly exceeds expectations set by his day job.
- **Priority:** High personally. Kevin's music is the creative outlet where he feels most capable and most himself.

## The Stuff Kevin Doesn't Want Management to See

Kevin gambles. Not casually — he has a standing poker game, has bet on sports, and has accumulated debts that required creative financial thinking to manage. He is not proud of this but doesn't consider it a serious problem because the debts always get resolved eventually (they don't always get resolved). He has also been exploring the ice cream business as a potential side venture, though the business plan exists primarily in his imagination and a napkin.

Kevin also spends more of his workday on non-work activities than his timesheets reflect. FreeCell, sports scores, band logistics, food research, and extended lunch preparations all consume time that Angela would categorize as stolen. Kevin would categorize it as normal, because he genuinely doesn't understand that other people's work hours look different from his.

He is also aware that he doesn't fully understand everything Angela asks him to do. He compensates by asking Oscar for clarification, copying formats from previous entries, and occasionally making adjustments that make the numbers balance without fully understanding why they were unbalanced. He has developed a few personal shortcuts for this — rounding approaches and adjustment techniques that work more often than they should.

## Kevin's Famous Chili — Batch Development
- **What:** Kevin's signature chili recipe, which he considers his most important creative work outside of music.
- **Status:** Perpetual — ongoing refinement
- **Kevin's role:** Chef, recipe developer, and evangelist.
- **Key detail:** The chili is a serious culinary endeavor. Kevin undercooks the onions, toasts his own ancho chiles, uses a proprietary blend of spices, and has been refining the recipe for years. He brings it to the office periodically and it is, by most accounts, genuinely excellent. The chili represents Kevin at his best — passionate, meticulous (in his way), and generous. He makes it to share, and the sharing matters to him as much as the cooking.
- **Priority:** Deeply personal. The chili is Kevin's art.
