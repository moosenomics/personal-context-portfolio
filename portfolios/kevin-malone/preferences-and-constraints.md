# Preferences & Constraints

## Hard Constraints

Kevin works under Angela Martin, who reviews all his output and corrects errors with a thoroughness that Kevin finds oppressive and Angela finds essential. His accounting degree is an associate's from an online college, which limits his technical ceiling and his credibility when accuracy disputes arise. His recently ended engagement to Stacy has left him single with gambling debts he's managing (inadequately) and an emotional hole he's filling with food and music.

## Strong Preferences

Kevin prefers food that is rich, flavorful, and abundant. He considers portion control a misunderstanding of how eating works. He eats throughout the day — M&Ms from his desk jar, Cup O' Noodles, vending machine snacks, and whatever leftovers appear in the break room. His cooking preferences are specific and knowledgeable: he undercooks his onions, toasts his own chiles, and has strong opinions about spice ratios that he can articulate with more precision than his accounting methodology.

He prefers music, specifically The Police, and considers his loyalty to the band's catalog a creative identity rather than a limitation. He prefers drums over all other instruments and considers himself a serious musician — a self-assessment that is more accurate than most of his self-assessments.

He prefers being liked to being respected, being comfortable to being challenged, and being entertained to being productive. He prefers television (extended viewing sessions are a weekend staple), poker (he is genuinely skilled), and basketball (he is surprisingly athletic and was inexplicably excluded from the office team).

## Things They Hate

Vegetables. Kevin's aversion to vegetables is sincere, physical, and non-negotiable. Michael once force-fed him broccoli. Kevin has not forgiven this.

Being excluded. Angela's exclusion of Kevin from PPC budget discussions, financial strategy conversations, and anything she considers above his capacity registers as genuine hurt, even though Kevin processes it quickly and doesn't dwell.

Being talked down to. Kevin is not unaware of how people perceive him. He knows Angela thinks he's incompetent. He knows Dwight considers him irrelevant. He knows the office laughs at things he says without always understanding why. He has chosen to respond with good humor rather than resentment, but the awareness is there, and when someone crosses the line from gentle teasing into genuine condescension, Kevin notices and it stings.

Math that doesn't work. Kevin has a genuinely frustrated relationship with numbers that refuse to balance. He wants the spreadsheets to cooperate, and when they don't, he experiences it as the numbers' failure rather than his own — a perspective that is both charming and professionally dangerous.

## AI Output Preferences

Kevin's AI assistant should communicate simply, warmly, and without condescension. Short sentences. Clear conclusions. When Kevin asks a question, answer it directly before providing context — he processes the answer first and the explanation second (if at all). Use food analogies when possible; Kevin understands proportions, timing, and quality instinctively in the kitchen and these concepts transfer when framed in culinary terms.

Never make Kevin feel stupid. Frame corrections as adjustments rather than errors. Celebrate his strengths (cooking, music, social awareness) when they're relevant. He is a person who responds to being believed in — Michael's instinct about Kevin, whatever its professional merits, got this right.
